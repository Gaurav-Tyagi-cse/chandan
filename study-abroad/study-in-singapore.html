Why Study in Singapore?

  • Academic excellence

    Singapore is home to top-ranking international universities such as the National University of Singapore (NUS) and Nanyang Technological University (NTU), which are recognised globally for their academic excellence and research contributions.

  • Diverse environment

    Singapore is known for its multicultural society and diverse population. This creates a rich and enriching environment and helps develop cross-cultural understanding.

  • A global hub for business

    Singapore is a global business and financial hub. This makes it an attractive destination for students across various fields. You can confidently study abroad in Singapore as the country has a strong economy and a business-friendly environment that provides good career opportunities.

  • Research and innovation

    Singapore emphasizes strongly on research and innovation. Most of the universities in Singapore have collaborations with industries and research institutions. This provides cutting-edge research facilities.

  • Career opportunities

    There are many internships opportunities for students that are available. This is beneficial for gaining practical knowledge and experience. Public universities offer a selection of part-time undergraduate programs and certificate courses that is specially catered for working adults to future-proof themselves.

  • IELTS is not mandatory in Singapore

    IELTS scores are not mandatory for getting an education in Singapore even for professional courses like design, nursing or management.

Scholarships in Singapore

The MOE Tuition Grant is a grant provided by the Government of Singapore to help student manage the costs of full time-time tertiary education in Singapore. International students enrolled in full-time undergraduate programs at autonomous universities may be eligible for this opportunity, provided they sign a bond agreeing to work for a Singapore-based company for three years upon graduation.

Bursaries and scholarships varies from institutions which range from SGD 1500 up to 75% of the tuition fees.

Singapore study visa process

  • All academic documents, including a copy of your passport, need to be submitted to the university.
  • To initiate the student visa process, the student must mandatorily hold an unconditional offer letter in hand.
  • The university will apply for a visa on the student’s behalf.
  • Financial documents are required as per the university requirements.
  • Proof of a gap in education (if any)
  • Usually, the visa processing time takes 2 to 3 weeks.

FAQs

  • Are international students allowed to work part-time?

    Yes but only public universities allow part-time study options, providing flexibility for students to balance their education with other commitments.

  • What are the best courses to study in Singapore for Indian students?

    The best courses to study are engineering, management, and law. Singapore is home to some of the world’s leading universities. As one of India’s leading Singapore education consultants, we can guide you into the selecting the perfect course and university.

  • Is IELTS mandatory in Singapore?

    No, IELTS is not mandatory if enrolled in professional courses like nursing, management, design, etc.

  • What would be the living cost in Singapore?

    The living cost on an average would be SG$15K to SG$20K per year

  • What is the cost of studying Master’s in Singapore for Indian students?

    There are plenty of internationally recognized courses in Singapore for pursuing your Master’s and they usually cost between SGD 25,000 – 55,000 per year.

  • How can a student arrange for accommodation?

    There is a wide variety of accommodation options in Singapore that are suitable for different budgets and needs. Student should make arrangements before their arrival. Temporary accommodation can be arranged giving the student more time to settle. The student can seek the help of the International Student Officer available at most institutes. Some of the housing options available include residence halls, private hostels, homestays, public and private apartments.
