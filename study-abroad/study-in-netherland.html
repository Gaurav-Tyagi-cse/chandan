Netherlands study visa process

  • To get the Netherlands student visa, Indian students will need the Long Study Visa (MVV) or resident permit. The visa is specially for students who are ready to start their study abroad journey in the Netherlands.
  • The immigration department takes approximately 60 to 90 days to process the MVV and resident permit, respectively. The concerned educational institutions are informed about the final decision.
  • In case of acceptance, an appointment has to be made with the Indian Embassy to collect the MVV and have the biometrics taken.
  • The deadline for collecting MVV is 3 months from the date of issue. However, it is valid for 90 days from the date of collection from the embassy.
  • An international student is allowed to travel in the Netherlands with a provisional residence permit.
  • A student needs to undergo a Tuberculosis test upon arrival as part of the entry procedure.

FAQs

  • Are there scholarships available for studying in the Netherlands?

    The Netherlands has a number of scholarships for international students studying at different levels. Here are some of the scholarships available: Holland Scholarship, Orange Knowledge Programme, and Radboud University Scholarship Programme, University of Maastricht High Potential Scholarship, Utrecht University Excellence Scholarships, TU Delft Excellence Scholarships, Leiden University Excellence Scholarships, Erik Bleumink Scholarships at the University of Groningen, University of Twente Scholarships.

  • Can I work while studying in the Netherlands?

    You are permitted to work in the Netherlands if you have a student residence permit. But during the year, your working hours are limited to 16 hours a week, or full-time during the summer (June to August).

  • Do I need to be fluent in Dutch to study in the Netherlands?

    No, it is not compulsory to learn Dutch. 95% of the people are English speakers and the universities offer plenty of courses that are offered entirely in English.

  • What is the cost of studying in the Netherlands?

    The cost of studying bachelor’s at the colleges in the Netherlands is 6,000 to 15,000 euros per year; for master’s, the cost is 8,000 to 20,000 euros per year. The cost of studying at a private university would be around 30,000 euros per year.

  • What are the best universities for studying a Master’s in the Netherlands?

    The best universities in the Netherlands for studying include Global School, Nyenrode Business School, University of Twente, University of Amesterdam, The Hague University of Applied Sciences, and Wittenbourg University of Applied Sciences.
