All About Pursuing MBA in Finance in the UK

The UK is one of the most sought-after study abroad destinations among many international students. Study in UK provides an enthralling academic experience that blends top-notch education with a diverse cultural experience. The UK, renowned for having outstanding universities, offers a vast array of programs across subjects that encourage critical thinking, research abilities, and a global perspective. Interacting with top researchers, using state-of-the-art research facilities, and prospering in a setting that encourages independent thought all benefit students. Furthermore, the UK's heterogeneous society allows for interaction with people from diverse backgrounds, increasing cross-cultural understanding and personal growth. The historical importance of the UK and it’s contemporary infrastructure provide students with an intriguing backdrop for exploration and personal and academic success.

Reasons to seek an MBA in Finance in the UK

For those wishing to further their careers in the financial industry, pursuing an MBA in Finance in the UK can provide a number of advantages and prospects.

  1. Esteemed Universities: The UK houses some of the most prestigious business schools and universities in the world. Top-notch MBA programs with a significant emphasis on finance are offered by universities like London Business School, The University of Cambridge, and The University of Oxford. A degree from one of these colleges can boost your status and open doors to global professional prospects.
  2. Global Exposure: Students the world over are drawn to the UK, fostering a global and diverse environment. Interacting with peers from diverse origins can provide useful insights into global finance practices while also helping to create a powerful worldwide network.
  3. Financial Centre: London, which is home to many banks, investment companies, and financial institutions, is a significant global financial hub. Studying near these institutions can provide unique chances for internships, networking, and exposure to real-world financial operations.
  4. Industry Links: Business schools in UK usually forge strong relationships with industries through organizing seminars, inviting guest lecturers, and sponsoring networking events. Through these possibilities, students can interact with finance professionals and gain knowledge about current trends and practices.
  5. In-depth Curriculum: In-depth finance curricula are frequently provided by MBA programs in the UK, encompassing topics like investment management, corporate finance, financial analysis, and risk management. This comprehensive approach prepares graduates for a wide range of careers in the finance industry.
  6. Employment Possibilities: Numerous financial careers are available in the UK, ranging from corporate finance and fintech to investment banking and asset management. A wide range of alternatives are available for MBA graduates thanks to the presence of startups and global organizations.
  7. Alumni Association: Typically, UK business schools have sizable global alumni networks. You can use these networks as helpful tools for mentorship, job leads, and career advice as you travel your post-MBA path.
  8. Research Possibilities: Many universities in UK have robust research programs in finance if that's something that interests you. Participating in research projects can improve one’s comprehension of financial concepts and advance expertise in the field.
  9. Developing Soft Skills: MBA programs emphasize the development of critical soft skills viz., leadership, communication, and problem-solving, essential for success in the finance industry, where good decision-making and communication are critical.
  10. Cultural Understanding: Discovering historical landmarks, cultural events, and a variety of cuisines are all made possible by studying in the UK. This can aid in one’s personal development and world viewpoint.

Eligibility Requirements to pursue an MBA in Finance in the UK

International students must fulfil the following eligibility requirements in order to enrol in an MBA in Finance at any of the UK business schools.

  1. A legitimate Bachelor's degree from a university that is recognized
  2. English Language Proficiency test results
  3. GRE/GMAT test scores
  4. 2-3 years of Professional Experience
  5. A current Passport and a Student Visa
  6. Statement of Purpose
  7. Recommendation Letters

Pursuing MBA in Finance in the UK: Costs

The price of an MBA in Finance program in the UK can vary widely based on the university, length of the program, location, and any additional expenses. The average cost of an MBA in Finance is between £31,500 and £97,500. Typically, private institutions charge higher tuition than public universities.

Your cost of living, however, will vary based on where you live in the UK. According to UK student visa criteria, the average cost of living in the UK, excluding London, is roughly £12,180 annually, or £1,015 monthly. While in London, students are expected to have £15,180 annually, or £1265 each month. These are the bare minimums that you must have in your bank account each month, however a student in the UK spends between £1500 and 1800 per month.

Employment Prospects in the UK post an MBA in Finance

A professional in the accounting and finance sector may earn about £73,100 a year. The average MBA finance income in the UK is £29,600, however with years of experience, that compensation can rise to £147,000. Following two to five years of experience, you should anticipate a salary increase of about 30%. Companies like Google, MasterCard, Siemens, IBM, HP, Amazon, Infosys, Accenture, and PwC accept graduates with an MBA in Finance from the UK.
