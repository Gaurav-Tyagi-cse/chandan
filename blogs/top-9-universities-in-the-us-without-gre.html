Top 9 Universities In The US Without GRE

The GRE General Test is a standardized test designed and regulated by the Educational Testing Service (ETS). GRE is a prerequisite for various technical master's degrees in countries like the USA.

Students from non-technical backgrounds, find GRE and apodous examination to crack. For many candidates with a good overall profile, an average GRE score blunts their prospects of securing admittance to an ostensible university. Therefore, many institutions are now discontinuing the need to take the GRE. Almost 70-80% of the universities in the US are offering programs without the GRE requirement.

The US is an abode to numerous top-ranked universities offering MS specializations without GRE scores.

FAQs:

For MS in the USA, is GRE a requirement?

As a result, it is critical to examine the exact requirements of each university and program you are interested in to discover whether the GRE is required.

Can we pursue an MS in USA without GRE and IELTS?

Yes, it is feasible to pursue MS in USA without GRE or IELTS tests. What is the minimal GRE score needed for MS in the United States?

In the USA, a GRE score of 310 is regarded as acceptable for MS. It typically suffices to gain admission into the majority of engineering or computer science programs offered in the nation. The program average is what counts; if it is between 315 and 320, then you should score higher than this to improve your chances of being admitted.

Is the GRE exempt in 2024?

Whether the GRE will be waived for Spring 2024 or Fall 2024 varies on individual university decisions and rules, while many colleges who skipped the GRE during the epidemic have continued to do so for future admittance cycles.

Without the GRE, how can universities like Purdue University, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, and Boston University evaluate applicants?

Universities without GRE in USA such as Purdue, MIT, and Boston University can examine applicants holistically, taking into account variables other than standardized test scores. These could include:

  1. Academic Standing: US universities without GRE will evaluate candidates' undergraduate transcripts, GPA, and coursework difficulty to determine their academic aptitude and accomplishments.
  2. Recommendation Letters: Letters of Recommendation from professors, mentors, or employers can provide insight on a candidate's morals, skills, and potential.
  3. SOP: An essay or personal statement outlining the applicant's academic preferences, professional objectives, and reasons for applying to the programme in question is frequently submitted by applicants.
  4. Work Experience: For some programs, a candidate's knowledge and suitability may be determined by their achievements and relevant job experience.
  5. Extracurricular Activities: Participation in extracurricular pursuits, volunteer work, or research initiatives can show a candidate's commitment and versatility.
  6. Interviews: Applicants' personalities, interests, and objectives may be better understood by institutions through interviews.

By utilizing a variety of evaluation techniques, universities can acquire a comprehensive understanding of candidates and make informed admission decisions.

Are there any substitute examinations that American universities that don't require the GRE accept?

For admittance to their graduate programs, some colleges in USA are beginning to waive the GRE (Graduate Record Examination) requirement. Instead, US universities without GRE may accept scores from other standardized tests or offer alternatives for candidates unwilling to submit their GRE results. However, remember that admissions standards might vary over time and that each university may have its own unique requirements.

Some common GRE alternatives that several universities without GRE in USA may accept include:

  1. GMAT (Graduate Management Admission Test): Typically used for graduate business programs viz. MBA programs.
  2. LSAT (Law School Admission Test): For enrollment in law schools and similar courses.
  3. MCAT (Medical College Admission Test): A prerequisite for entrance to medical schools and associated health professions programmes.
  4. GRE Subject Tests: Scores from the GRE Subject Test, which evaluates candidates' subject knowledge, may be accepted by several universities.
  5. TOEFL or IELTS: For international students whose primary language is not English.
  6. Work Experience: For applicants with substantial job experience, several universities may skip the GRE requirement.
  7. Academic Success: GRE scores may also be substituted for exceptionally strong undergraduate academic performance.

Visit the institution's official website or get in touch with the admissions office for the most recent entrance standards and alternative examinations that are approved. Institutional and departmental policies may vary.
