All About Pursuing Social Sciences in Ireland

Owing to its globally-acclaimed universities, rich cultural legacy, and hospitable setting, Ireland is a well-liked study aboad destination that provides students with a distinct blend of academic prowess and lively social interactions.

In Ireland, Social Sciences programs generally cover subjects viz., psychology, economics, political science, and sociology, that provide a thorough grasp of societal structures and human behavior. Undergraduate degrees typically take 3-4 years to complete, while master's programs last 1-2 years.

Why study Social Sciences in Ireland?

One should consider studying Social Sciences in Ireland for the below mentioned reasons:

  • First-rate Instruction: Ireland is home to prestigious universities with excellent programs in social science.
  • English Language: Programs offered in English to improve global communication abilities.
  • Cultural Exposure: Students studying in Ireland gain exposure to a rich cultural legacy and a varied society.
  • Historical Relevance: Study in a country with important political and historical events.
  • Contemporary Amenities: Students gain access to cutting-edge facilities and resources.
  • Possibilities for Research: Availability of state-of-the-art research and partnerships.
  • Friendly Setting: Hospitable and encouraging communities for overseas students.
  • Government Assistance: There are numerous scholarships and funding possibilities available to students.
  • Worldwide Viewpoint: Exposure to overseas students and global challenges.
  • Employment Prospects: Ireland boasts a robust labor market with linkages to numerous industries.

What's the cost of pursuing Social Sciences in Ireland?

Based on the university and the degree of study, the cost of pursuing Social Sciences in Ireland differs. For undergraduate programs, international students generally pay between €12,000-€20,000 in tuition annually. On the contrary, EU students typically pay lower tuition fees usually about €3,000 annually, nevertheless this can differ depending on the particular university and program.

In comparison to undegraduate programs, postgraduate programs are generally more expensive, with overseas students usually paying between €12,000 and €24,000 per year. Moreover, students should set aside money for living expenses, which based on the city and his/her way of living can range from €10,000-€15,000 annually. To assist in alleviating these expenses, part-time work opportunities, scholarships, and other forms of monetary assistance may be available.

Eligibility Criteria to pursue Social Sciences in Ireland

The eligibility criteria to pursue Social Sciences in Ireland are:

  • The student needs to complete secondary education with pertinent subjects. Students pursuing undergraduate programs generally require a Leaving Certificate or comparable. A pertinent Bachelor’s degree is needed for postgraduate programs.
  • Submission of academic transcripts from education institutions previously attended.
  • He/she should fulfill the minimum grade prerequisites established by the institution. Competitive GPA for postgraduate programs.
  • International students need to prove their fluency in English through appearing for English language proficiency tests viz., the IELTS (minimum overall score of 6.5), TOEFL, or comparable.
  • Particular postgraduate programs may demand pertinent work experience.
  • Submission of a research proposal (for research-oriented programs).
  • The student needs to submit a statement of purpose or personal statement.
  • Recommendation letters.
  • Curriculum Vitae (CV) or resume.
  • The student may need to appear for an entrance exam or interview (if applicable).
  • Evidence of adequate funding.
  • Filled-out application form.
  • Non-EU/EEA citizens require a valid student visa.
  • Students should examine the university’s official website for particular admission prerequisites.

FAQs:

Are there any scholarships available for social science studies in Ireland?

Yes, there are various scholarships offered to both undergraduate and postgraduate students. These entail university-specific scholarships, government scholarships, and scholarships offered by private organizations.

Does applying for a postgraduate program require one to have a background in social sciences?

Although having a background in social sciences is frequently preferred, students from other fields are also accepted by a number of programs, given they satisfy the academic and other prerequisites established by the university.

While studying social sciences in Ireland, are overseas students allowed to work?

Yes, overseas students on a student visa are permitted to work part-time for a maximum of 20 hours per week during the academic term and full-time for a maximum of 40 hours per week during holidays.
