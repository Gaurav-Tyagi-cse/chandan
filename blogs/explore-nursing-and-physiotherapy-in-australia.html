Explore Nursing and Physiotherapy in Australia

Nursing and physiotherapy are a dynamic field in Australia that has a vibrant and ever-evolving landscape. The industry continues to grow steadily with the rise in population and varying ailments in the population. The universities here put a strong emphasis on high-quality education for their international and national students. As nurses and physiotherapists play an important role in the healthcare system, studying the same would give international industry exposure, gain new skills, provide different perspectives, etc. As the industry grows with time, the demand for job opportunities in the market might continue to grow. Keep reading to learn more about nursing and physiotherapy in Australia.

Nursing or physiotherapy-which one is better?

Nursing:

  • There is no specific age limit to pursue nursing as a program. There are many track courses available in Australia for accelerating to the new workforce.
  • Nursing has a lot of specialties to choose from. Public health, community health settings are some of the specific areas.
  • The demand for nurses is increasing as the awareness for healthcare increases. Over the next 10 to 15 years, as the world's population continues to rise, there will probably be an even greater scarcity of nurses. Additionally, as more people age and become older than ever before, there is a greater need than ever for qualified nurses to improve patient satisfaction, raise quality of life, and deliver higher standards of care, particularly in rural and isolated locations.
  • Working in nursing allows you to work flexible hours. Your hours are flexible and can range from 4 to 12 hours on weekdays, evenings, and weekends, whether you work full-time or part-time.

Physiotherapist:

  • Physiotherapists play an important role by working in various healthcare settings like hospitals, private clinics, rehabilitation centres, aged care centres, sports clinics, community healthcare facilities, etc.
  • Physiotherapists are essential in maintaining good health and preventing accidents and impairments by means of occupational health programmes, education, ergonomic evaluations, and exercise regimens.
  • Australia boasts a robust healthcare research culture that offers physiotherapists the chance to participate in cutting-edge research in fields including movement science, pain management, and rehabilitation, as well as evidence-based treatment.
  • Physiotherapy allows one to explore various paths, like musculoskeletal, neurological, paediatric, sports, and geriatric physiotherapy.

Types of physiotherapy and nursing programs

Before choosing the right programme, it is necessary to know the different types of programmes for both physiotherapy and nursing. Here are some of the common programmes that can be found at Australian universities:

Physiotherapy Programmes

  • Bachelor of Physiotherapy (B.Phty./B.Physio)
  • Master of Physiotherapy Practice (M.PhtyPrac)
  • Doctor of Physiotherapy (DPT).
  • Graduate Entry Master of Physiotherapy (GEMP)

Nursing Programmes:

  • Bachelor of Nursing (BN/BNSc)
  • Master of Nursing (MN/MNSc)
  • Doctor of Nursing (DN/DNSc)
  • Postgraduate Diploma in Nursing

Entry requirements

Physiotherapy:

  • Applicants must have passed high school or equivalent (in the case of a bachelor's program) with English, Chemistry, Biology, Mathematics, or Physics as subjects, or they should have completed a bachelor’s degree in a relevant field (in the case of a master’s program).
  • Demonstrate the scores of IELTS or TOEFL.
  • For pursuing physiotherapy abroad, relevant work experience is required in healthcare settings, such as volunteering or shadowing a physiotherapist.
  • Students need to submit a statement of purpose, letters of recommendation, and relevant documents at the time of admission.

Nursing programme:

  • Applicants need to have completed secondary education or a bachelor's with a strong academic record.
  • Students need to demonstrate evidence of immunisations and vaccines for safe participation in clinical placements.
  • Students need to submit a statement of purpose, letters of recommendation, and relevant documents at the time of admission.
  • Additionally, international students need to submit English language programmes such as IELTS and TOEFL with a minimum score set by the university.
  • Additionally, nursing students might require a police clearance checkup for working with children under clinical placements and with other vulnerable populations.
