Construct Your Ideal Future With a Business Administration Degree from the Netherlands

Owing to its top-tier education system, with a number of English-taught programs, and its hospitable, multicultural setting, the Netherlands is an alluring study abroad destination.

Generally placing a strong emphasis on international business, innovation, and management, Business Administration programs in the Netherlands are frequently instructed in English both at the undergraduate and graduate levels. Bachelor’s programs in Business Administration typically span 3 years, while Master’s programs usually take 1-2 years to complete, based on the particular course and university.

Why study Business Administration in the Netherlands?

Studying Business Administration in the Netherlands is a popular choice for the following reasons:

  • First-Rate Education: Universities in Netherlands are renowned for their stringent academic standards and routinely rank among the best worldwide.
  • Programs Instructed in English: A number of Business Administration programs are available in English, making them accessible to overseas students.
  • Budget-friendly Tuition Fees: In comparison to other Western European nations, the Netherlands offers comparatively inexpensive tuition fees and living costs.
  • Cutting-edge Methods of Instruction: In business education, there is an emphasis on hands-on learning, case studies, and real-world applications.
  • Standard of Living: The Netherlands boasts an excellent standard of living, safety, and outstanding public services.
  • Central Location: The country’s strategic location in Europe offers seamless access to other major European cities, thereby, increasing travel and business prospects.
  • Global Setting: Home to a varied population, the Netherlands provides a multicultural encounter and the chance to establish a worldwide network.
  • Robust Assistance for Startups: The Netherlands boasts a thriving startup ecosystem that offers resources and assistance for entrepreneurial students.
  • Strong Business Centre: The Netherlands is a significant European business hub, offering students a wealth of internship possibilities and connections to international firms.
  • Outstanding Employment Opportunities: Graduates from universities in Netherlands are in high demand, both within the country and globally.

What’s the cost of studying Business Administration in the Netherlands?

Based on the type of institution and the student’s nationality, the cost of studying Business Administration in the Netherlands differs. EU/EEA students usually pay between €2,500-€4,500 in tuition per year for undergraduate programs. The tuition fees for non-EU/EEA students are higher, usually costing between €6,000-€15,000 annually for undergraduate programs. On the contrary, master’s programs charge higher tuition fees, with EU/EEA students paying between €2,500 and €5,500 per year, whereas non-EU/EEA students may pay tuition ranging from €8,000-€20,000 or more annually. Moreover, students should set aside money for living costs, which can cost between €800-€1,200 per month, and cover food, housing, transit, and other individual expenditures.

Eligibility Criteria to study Business Administration in the Netherlands

The eligibility criteria to pursue Business Administration in the Netherlands are:

  • The student needs to have completed secondary education that is comparable to the Dutch VWO (pre-university education). For master’s programs, the student needs to hold a bachelor’s degree often in Business Administration, Economics, or an associated field.
  • He/she should submit academic transcripts from all education institutions previously attended.
  • The applicant needs to have a strong GPA, frequently comparable to a Dutch grade of 7.0 or higher. Particular grade prerequisites in pertinent subjects such as mathematics may apply to certain programs.
  • For MBA programs, the student needs to submit scores of standardized tests like the GMAT or GRE.
  • Non-native English speakers need to prove their fluency in English through appearing for English language proficiency tests like the IELTS or TOEFL.
  • Certain programs, especially MBA programs, may require students to possess pertinent work experience.
  • Submission of a motivation letter or personal statement describing the student’s interest in the program and professional objectives.
  • 1 or more recommendation letters from instructors or professionals (as required by certain programs).
  • A comprehensive curriculum vitae (CV) showcasing the student’s scholastic achievements, and extracurricular activities.
  • As part of the selection procedure, the student may need to appear for an interview.
  • Additional entrance tests or assessments may be required by certain universities.
  • Non-EU/EEA students must apply for a student visa and residence permit.

FAQs:

  1. Are scholarships available?

    Yes, scholarships for overseas students are offered by numerous universities and outside entities. For information on available options and application details, students should visit the website of the particular university.

  2. Can international students work while studying?

    Yes, overseas students are permitted to work part-time for a maximum of 16 hours per week during the academic year and full-time during vacations.
