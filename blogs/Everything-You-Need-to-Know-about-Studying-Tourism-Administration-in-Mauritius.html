Everything You Need to Know about Studying Tourism Administration in Mauritius

Mauritius is a hidden gem located in the Indian Ocean near the lower eastern side of the African continent. Over the past few years it has had a huge surge in its tourism industry and is today known for its pristine beaches, lush landscapes, and the vibrant cultural heritage which is a mix of African, Indian, Chinese, and European influences The attempt at keeping up with the increased demand for tourists has resulted in the rise of luxurious resorts providing world-class hospitality and diverse attractions ranging from adventurous water sports to thrilling nature trails. The government’s focus on sustainable tourism and the enhancement of infrastructure has further bolstered its appeal in the international sustainability-focused market, making Mauritius a paradise for tourists from every part of the world.

Why Study Tourism Administration in Mauritius?

  • Studying in Mauritius places you in the heart of a thriving tourism industry known for its stunning landscapes and luxury resorts. The industry continues to grow year-on-year allowing students to be at the centre of a bustling tourist destination that attracts a huge number of tourists per year.
  • Mauritius’s rich cultural mix and multilingual environment provide a unique backdrop for understanding the varied preferences and expectations of international tourists, enhancing your ability to cater to a global clientele and their unique desires.
  • The tourism administration programs that are taught in Mauritius often include practical components and case studies that reflect current challenges and opportunities in the tourism sector, giving you exposure to real world problems instead of simulations based on old methods.
  • The growing tourism industry in Mauritius is always looking for fresh talent and local universities and institutions frequently partner with leading tourism businesses, providing you with exclusive internship opportunities and direct pathways to employment in a flourishing market.
  • Mauritius’s strategic location in the Indian Ocean offers the students a unique vantage point to observe and analyze global tourism trends, preparing them to adapt to emerging patterns in the industry straight out from college.
  • The curriculum is designed to reflect the latest trends and technological advancements in tourism, ensuring that you acquire contemporary skills and knowledge that are highly relevant to the current job market.
  • Nothing is better than studying in an environment that is both serene and inspiring, and Mauritius provides exactly that with its picturesque locations that make your education over there not only enriching but also enjoyable and stress free.

Top Universities in Mauritius to Pursue Tourism Administration

In Mauritius, several universities and institutions offer programs in tourism administration or related fields. Here are some of the top options:

  1. University of Mauritius (UOM)
  2. Mauritius Institute of Training and Development (MITD)
  3. Open University of Mauritius (OU)
  4. Université des Mascareignes
  5. Institut Supérieur de Management (ISM)

What’s the Cost of Pursuing Tourism Administration in Mauritius?

Private institutions have fees ranging from MUR 100,000 to MUR 200,000 per year for a bachelor’s degree in tourism administration. Master’s programs generally have higher tuition fees, typically ranging from MUR 150,000 to MUR 250,000 per year. The total living expenses, including rent, utilities, conveyance, and transportation, will amount to approximately MUR 16,000 per month. Additionally, international students should factor in health insurance costs.

Job Opportunities after Studying Tourism Administration in Mauritius

  • Tourism Manager: Managing the operations of hotels, resorts, or tour companies. They are also responsible for overseeing all aspects of the various daily activities to ensure smooth and efficient functioning.
  • Travel Agent: Tourists need help with booking tickets and making an itinerary that includes the maximum number of places/experiences within the available amount of time and a travel agent takes care of all of that for a smooth travel experience.
  • Destination Marketing Manager: A large part of what makes a place a tourist attraction is the marketing that goes behind it and it’s the job of the destination marketing manager to make a destination shine. They handle all the marketing campaigns, partnerships, and public relations efforts.
  • Hotel and Resort Management: Working in hotel and resort management involves ensuring that the customers get the most comfortable and luxurious accommodation for their vacations.
  • Tour Guide: A tour guide has to be deeply knowledgeable about the local attractions, history, and culture of the place that they are guiding the tourists through which ensure a maximally engaing experience for the visitors.
  • Hospitality Consultant: This involves advising businesses on improving their hospitality services, operations, and customer experience and making sure that these businesses keep up with the latest trends in hospitality.
  • Cruise Ship Director: A cruise ship director oversees the operations and guest experience on cruise ships, including coordinating the various activities, managing staff, and ensuring high standards of service so that entire duration of stay in the sea is worth every penny of the tourist.

FAQs:

Q: What are the language requirements for studying in Mauritius?

Most programs are offered in English, so a student who wants to study abroad in Mauritius in one of these programs will have to prove English proficiency by taking a test like IELTS or TOEFL.

Q:What are the typical admission requirements for international students?

Usually a student needs to have the following for studying in a university in Mauritius,

    Proof of previous academic qualifications (high school equivalent certificate, bachelor’s degree, etc.) English proficiency test scores (e.g., IELTS, TOEFL) if English is not your first language. A personal statement, also known as a statement of purpose (SOP). Letters of recommendation (either academic or professional). Passport copy and visa application.
