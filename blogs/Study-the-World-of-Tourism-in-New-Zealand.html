Study the World of Tourism in New Zealand

The world of travel and tourism serves as a gateway to exploring the richness of tourism through the lens of New Zealand. With lush beauty, diverse cultural heritage, and boundless adventure opportunities, New Zealand stands as an ideal destination for those who wish to study travel and tourism.

Why study tourism in New Zealand?

  1. Globally recognised qualifications from universities: All 8 universities in New Zealand consistently rank well in QS world rankings and Times Higher ranking rankings. Most of these universities open doors to many prospects across the globe. It ensures that the institutions are registered in order to enrol and comply with international standards.
  2. Focus on research-based learning: New Zealand’s education system is influenced by the UK's education system. It focuses on research-based learning while dealing with it in a systematic manner. Studying in a country like New Zealand will teach one to think critically, solve problems, and benefit the most from student-teacher interaction. The students will get a chance to express the knowledge gained. New Zealand has been providing a lot of opportunities for its international students.
  3. A safe and peaceful nation: New Zealand ranks fourth on the Global Peace Index 2023. This speaks a lot about New Zealand in terms of social tolerance and the political stability of Kiwi democracy. As an international student or citizen, one will have the same rights as a Kiwi citizen.
  4. Good quality of life: New Zealand has multi-cultural environments, and the people here exist in harmony. There are many natural landscapes, including snow-capped mountains and volcanoes. Living here will give you an experience of natural beauty and culture.

The best universities offering travel and tourism

University of Waikato: The University of Waikato has strong ties with the tourism and hospitality industries in New Zealand and all around the globe. The programme here focuses on practical experience gained through internships and work acements. This allows the students to focus on their studies while having hands-on experience, theoretical knowledge, and practical knowledge. The curriculum has a strong focus on sustainable tourism practices, global trends, and the tourism industry.

University of Otago: The university is known for its innovative curriculum,which blends theoretical and practical knowledge. The various types of programmes here include a Bachelor of Commerce (BCom) in Tourism, a Master of Tourism, and a PhD in Tourism. This allows the students to pursue and choose from a wide range of programmes in the field. Also, the university puts strong emphasis on research. The students have the opportunity to engage in projects and tourism studies as a whole.

University of Auckland: The University of Auckland is the highest-ranking university in New Zealand, with globally recognised academic standards and research outcomes. Programmes here often come with a blend of business, environmental science, and cultural studies, giving students a broad perspective on tourism. Situated in the largest city and Tousim HB, the city has numerous attractions, thus making it an ideal location for studying Abrod.

Victoria University of Wellington:Victoria University of Wellington is renowned for its academic prowess and high standards of teaching and research. The university consistently rates highly in worldwide university rankings. The institution provides a variety of tourism programmes, including a Bachelor of Tourism Management, a Master of Tourism Management, and a PhD in Tourism Management. These programmes address a wide range of tourist issues, including management, policy, and planning. Victoria University of Wellington places a significant emphasis on research, allowing students to participate in cutting-edge research initiatives. The university's tourism research is internationally recognised, notably in areas like sustainable tourism and destination management.

Massey University:Massey University is one of New Zealand's major institutions, known for its research and teaching prowess.Bachelor of Business with a Major in Tourism Management: This programme combines business foundations with specialised tourism courses to prepare students for a variety of careers in the tourism industry. This programme offers students the opportunity the opportunity to explore further into tourism management, policy, and development, with opportunities for both study and practical implementation. The institution has significant relationships with industry partners, including tourism companies, government agencies, and non-governmental organisations, which allow for guest lectures, field visits, and collaborative projects. The curriculum combines ideas of sustainable tourism, preparing students to work in an industry that values environmental and social responsibility.

What is the Cost of Studying Tourism in New Zealand?

Studying tourism in New Zealand as an international student involves tuition fees that generally range from NZD 15,000 to NZD 30,000 per year, depending on the institution and program. Living expenses, including accommodation, food, transportation, health insureance, etc., typically amount between NZD 15,000 to NZD 25,000 per year. Students may avail scholarships and grants which will significantly reduce the cost of studying in New Zealand.

Eligibility criteria

Studying travel and tourism in New Zealand offers a lot of opportunities for its international students. This would vary as per the level of the programme and the stitution. Here are some general eligibility requirements for studying travel and tourism in New Zealand:

  • A high school diploma or equivalent or completion of secondary education or equivalent is required if applying for a bachelor’s programme.
  • A university graduate degree from a relevant institution is required if applying for a bachelor’s programme.
  • Relevant work experience in the field
  • The candidate should have applied to English language proficiency tests like IELTS, TOEFL, PTE, or Cambridge English.
  • Students should showcase proof of sufficient funds to cover tuition fees and living expenses.
  • In addition to all this, medical examinations, police certificates, and proof of good character have to be submitted 24 months in advance for admission purposes.
  • Additional documents like academic transcripts, proof of English language proficiency, personal statements, and statements of purpose have to be submitted as part of the admissions process.
