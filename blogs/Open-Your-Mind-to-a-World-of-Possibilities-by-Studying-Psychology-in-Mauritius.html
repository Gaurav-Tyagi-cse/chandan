Open Your Mind to a World of Possibilities by Studying Psychology in Mauritius

Mauritius is a small island nation located to the east of the African continent. The island's culture is a melting pot of African, Indian, Chinese, and European influences, reflected in its festivals, cuisine, and language. Creole, French, and English are the languages that are most commonly spoken here. The Mauritian people are known for their warmth and hospitality making any visitor, be it a tourist or a student feel immediately at home.

Economically, Mauritius has evolved from a sugar-dependent economy to a more diversified one, with thriving sectors in tourism, textiles, and financial services, positioning itself as a stable and dynamic player in the region. The financial services industry, in particular, has seen significant growth, with Mauritius positioning itself as a financial hub for the region. The natural beauty of Mauritius also draws in tourists from around the world. This scenic allure has bolstered its tourism industry, which, along with the burgeoning financial sector, has significantly contributed to the nation's economic growth.

What’s the Cost of Pursuing Psychology in Mauritius?

At public universities like the University of Mauritius, tuition fees are generally more affordable, ranging from MUR 10,000 to MUR 40,000 per year for undergraduate programs. In contrast, tuition fees in private instiutions can range from MUR 50,000 to MUR 100,000 per year or more. The total living expenses including rent, utilities, conveyance will come around to approximately MUR 16,000 per month.
