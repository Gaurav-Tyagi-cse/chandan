Discover the World of Hospitality and Tourism by Studying in Malaysia

Malaysia's strategic location in Southeast Asia and its blend of modern cities and natural beauty attract millions of visitors annually. From the bustling streets of Kuala Lumpur and the historic charm of George Town to the pristine beaches of Langkawi and the lush rainforests of Borneo, Malaysia offers a diverse range of experiences for the temporary visitor, be it for leisure or for business.

The hospitality sector, encompassing luxury hotels, boutique resorts, and traditional guest-houses, is known for its exceptional service and warm hospitality. The country’s hotels range from opulent, skyscraper accommodations to charming, hidden boutique stays nestled in lush rainforests. This is what makes Malaysia a prime ground for studying hospitality and tourism as Malaysia has it all when it comes to providing the finest tourist experience while the industry keeps growing year over year.

Why Study Hospitality and Tourism in Malaysia?

  • Diverse Tourism Market:The attractive travel destinations in Malaysia are diverse and offer a wide range of services all the way up to the highest level.
  • Strategic Location: Malaysia’s location in Southeast Asia provides a gateway to explore and understand tourism dynamics in the broader regional context.
  • Growing Industry: The hospitality and tourism sector has continued to grow year over year in Malaysia.
  • Cultural Exposure: Malaysia’s multicultural society offers unique insights into how differently each country approaches the task of customer service.
  • Educational Excellence: Reputable Malaysian institutions provide high-quality hospitality and tourism programs along with the required industry connection that will come handy when the student is ready to join the workforce.
  • Affordable Education: Malaysia offers a cost-effective education compared to many Western countries.
  • Government Support: The government has been making the push to grow and develop the hospitality industry as it is a good source of revenue for the country which means various programs and opportunities for students.
  • Practical Experience: A thriving hospitality sector also means that there will be plenty of Internship and practical training opportunities in Malaysia where students can get actual experience and understand what it means to work in the industry.
  • Networking Opportunities: Once you are employed and working you will have access to valuable network of professionals through industry events, conferences, and business partnerships.

What’s the Cost of Pursuing Hospitality and Tourism in Malaysia?

The cost of pursuing a degree in hospitality and tourism in Malaysia typically ranges from MYR 20,000 to MYR 45,000 per year for undergraduate programs. The accommodation costs varies based on if the student wishes to stay on-campus or off-campus. On-campus accommodation costs typically between MYR 200 to MYR 600 per month whereas off-campus accommodation costs between MYR 500 to MYR 1,500 per month. The total living expenses including rent will be approximately MYR 18,000 per year.

Eligibility Criteria to Pursue Hospitality and Tourism in Malaysia

To apply for undergraduate programs, you need to have completed higher secondary education (12th standard). For postgraduate programs, a relevant bachelor's degree is required, along with proof of English proficiency(IELTS/TOEFL) if the program is in English.

When applying, you usually need to fill out an application form, provide your academic transcripts, and submit a Statement of Purpose. If you’re applying for a postgraduate program, you’ll also need to include a CV or resume and letters of recommendation.

International students will also need to show proof of acceptance into a recognized institution, demonstrate financial stability, and have a health insurance coverage.

Job Opportunities after Studying Hospitality and Tourism in Malaysia

  1. Hotel Manager : Overseeing the daily operations of hotels, managing staff, and ensuring guest satisfaction.
  2. Event Coordinator : Planning and organizing events such as conferences, weddings, and corporate functions.
  3. Tour Guide : Leading tours and providing information about attractions and comprehensive information about the local culture to visitors.
  4. Restaurant Manager : Mange the day-to-day operations of a restaurants of both front and back end – like quality control, finances, managing customers etc.
  5. Front Desk Agent : Handling guest check-ins and check-outs at hotels, addressing inquiries, and providing concierge services.
  6. Sales and Marketing Executive : Developing marketing strategies and promotions to attract and retain customers in the hospitality sector.
  7. Cruise Line Staff : Working on cruise ships in various roles such as guest services, entertainment, and hospitality management.
