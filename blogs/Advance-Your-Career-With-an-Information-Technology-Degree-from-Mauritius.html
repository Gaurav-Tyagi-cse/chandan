Advance Your Career With an Information Technology Degree from Mauritius

Owing to its top-tier eduction system, stunning setting, and varied cultural exposure, Mauritius is a highly sought-after study abroad destination. Universities in Mauritius provide a myriad of English-taught programs, making it available to overseas students.

Information Technology programs in Mauritius are available at three different levels, including diploma, undergraduate, and postgraduate. Diplomas in information technology generally span 1-2 years, bachelor’s degrees generally take 3-4 years to complete, and master’s degrees typically last 1-2 years.

Why study Information Technology in Mauritius?

One should think about studying Information Technology in Mauritius for these compelling reasons:

  • Global Acknowledgement: Mauritius is home to recognized universities that provide internationally acknowledged credentials.
  • Budget-friendly: In comparison to other regions, the tuition costs and living expenses in Mauritius are reasonable.
  • Outstanding Education: Students who study in Mauritius gain access to state-of-the-art amenities and innovative technologies.
  • Cultural Exposure: Varied setting and distinct cultural experiences.
  • Assistance from the Government: The government has come up with projects to foster information technology (IT) growth and innovation.
  • Central Location: Mauritius has been established as a technology hub in the Indian Ocean.
  • Worldwide Prospects: Skills can be transferred worldwide.
  • Possibilities for Networking: Affiliations with regional as well as global technology communities.
  • Employment Opportunities: There is a substantial demand for IT specialists both locally and worldwide.

What’s the cost of studying Information Technology in Mauritius?

Based on the institution and degree of program, the cost of pursuing Information Technology in Mauritius differs. Typically, undergraduate courses cost between MUR 60,000-MUR 150,000 in tuition annually. Postgraduate courses on the other hand, range from MUR 80,000-MUR 200,000 in tuition per year. Moreover, students should set aside money for additional costs like textbooks, housing, and living expenses, which can increase the total cost of their education.

Eligibility Criteria to study Information Technology in Mauritius

The eligibility criteria to study Information Technology in Mauritius are:

  • For undergraduate programs, the student needs to hold a high school diploma or its comparable with a solid background in mathematics and science.
  • For master’s programs, the student needs to hold a pertinent bachelor’s degree.
  • The student needs to submit academic transcripts from all education institutions previously attended.
  • Based on the institution, he/she must meet particular grade prerqeuisites.
  • Scores of standardized tests or entrance exams may be required by certain programs.
  • If the student’s prior education was not in English, he/she may need to prove his/her fluency in the language through appearing for English lanuguage proficiency tests viz., the IELTS or TOEFL.
  • Submission of a personal statement.
  • Recommendation letters.
  • The student may also need to appear for an interview (as required by certain institutions).
  • The student may need to provide evidence of adequate finances to pay for tution costs and living expenses.
  • Submission of the filled-out application form.
  • The student needs to hold a current passport and student visa application.
