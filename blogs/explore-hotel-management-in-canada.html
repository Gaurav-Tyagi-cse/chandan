Explore Hotel Management in Canada

Hotel management as a discipline stands out as having a highly demanding scope. The industry in Canada is a great choice for students with a flair for professional development. Graduates in hotel management are the most sought-after by employers across Canada and the world. The hospitality and tourism sector, which employs over 1.8 million people, is one of the biggest employers in Canada and contributes significantly to the nation's continuous economic growth. Furthermore, it is anticipated that until 2028, there will be 43,400 open positions in the hospitality and tourist sectors in Canada, making it the most sought-after country for higher education.

Why study hotel management in Canada?

  1. Good job opportunities: Canadian collegesin Canada provide good job opportunities after graduation. The hotel management sector has huge demand in Canada as well as around the world.
  2. Good education: Along with good infrastructure, Canada has different hospitality and tourism programmes that stand out in the world. Master’s in hospitality management in Canada and diplomas in hotel management are some of the popular courses to study at Canadian universities.
  3. Future: Being in a hotel management role in the sector has immense benefits in Canada as well as internationally. Job Bank Canada estimates that there will be 43,400 new positions for hotel managers in Canada, with around 42,700 job seekers.
  4. Innovative Hospitality Sector: Adapting to the ever-changing demands and preferences of tourists, Canada's hospitality sector is dynamic and inventive.
  5. Diverse and Inclusive Environment: Students in Canada come from a variety of backgrounds, and the country is renowned for its friendly and cosmopolitan community. Students can engage with people from different backgrounds, cultures, and viewpoints while studying hotel management in Canada. This creates a dynamic and welcoming learning environment that encourages cross-cultural understanding and global awareness.

Top hotel management degrees and specialisations in Canada

Canadian institutions offer diverse degrees and specialisations within the hospitality industry. Here are the top management degrees and specialisations in Canada:

  • Bachelor of Commerce in Hospitality and Tourism Management
  • Bachelor of Tourism Management
  • Diploma in hotel management.
  • Master of Science in Hospitality and Tourism Management
  • Certificate Programmes in Hospitality Management

Additionally, there are various specialisations in hotel management programmes, such as event management, culinary arts, spa management, resort management, and revenue management.

The cost of studying hotel management in Canada

The cost of studying hotel management varies as per the length of the program. The cost of diploma and undergraduate programmes is up to CAD 20,000 per year. Whereas the cost of studying for a for a master's in hotel management is CAD 30,000 to CAD 60,000 per year.

Eligibility criteria

  • Entrance exam (as per the course and university requirements) followed by group discussion and personal interviews.
  • GRE/GMAT scores if applying for an MBA in hotel management.
  • International students will have to submit IELTS, TOEFL, and PTE.
  • For a B.Com. in hotel management, one must score a minimum 50% in the 12th. In the case of a PG applicant, a bachelor’s degree would be required.

Trends and challenges in the hotel management industry in Canada

Trends:

  • With technology entering the hotel management sector, hotels choose to have mobile check-ins and check-outs, keyless entry systems, and guest service apps to enhance the guest experience and streamline operations. Also, there are advancements in data analytics and artificial intelligence that enhance the user experience by personalising services, optimising pricing strategies, and improving operational efficiency.
  • Consumer preferences and expectations are constantly evolving through demographic shifts, cultural influences, and changing lifestyles. The majority hotels in Canada innovate and adapt as per the diverse needs of guests. This incorporates local culture and cuisine while providing seamless interactions.

Concerns:

  • The hotel management sector is becoming more and more concerned with sustainability, as Canadian hotels are adopting eco-friendly measures to reduce their environmental impact. This covers projects like water conservation efforts, waste reduction plans, energy-efficient building designs, and buying locally made goods. Additionally, travellers are looking for more environmentally friendly lodging, which is driving demand for eco-friendly certifications and sustainable hotels.
  • The Canadian hotel management business continues to face considerable issues related to talent management and labour shortages. The hotel industry has a high demand for professional people, including chefs, front-line staff, and managers, but it has trouble finding and keeping talent. Labour shortages are caused by several factors, including seasonal variations, competition from other industries, and shifting demographics.
