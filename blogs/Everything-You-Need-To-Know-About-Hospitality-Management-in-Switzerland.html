Everything You Need To Know About Hospitality Management in Switzerland

With its top-notch education system, varied cultural setting, and stunning landscapes, Switzerland provides an enticing study abroad experience. Overseas students benefit from globally acclaimed universities, multilingual possibilities, and a strong focus on research and innovation.

Frequently integrating theoretical knowledge with hands-on experience, Hospitality Management programs in Switzerland offer first-rate instruction in luxury and service excellence. Undergraduate degrees in Hospitality Management generally take 3-4 years to complete whereas master’s degrees typically span 1-2 years. Diplomas and Certificates can range from 6 months to one year.

Why study Hospitality Management in Switzerland?

Students should think about studying Hospitality Management in Switzerland for these compelling reasons:

  • Internationally Acclaimed Universities: Renowned hospitality schools viz., Glion Institute of Higher Education and Ecole hôtelière de Lausanne (EHL), recognized for their stringent academic standards and industrial links are located in Switzerland.
  • Top-tier Instruction: Frequently integrating theoretical knowledge with real-world experience, hospitality programs in Switzerland provide a thorough understanding of the industry.
  • International Acknowledgement: Degrees earned from universities in Switzerland are highly valued globally, which might be beneficial in the worlwide labor market.
  • Cutting-edge Syllabus: In order to equip students for the difficulties of the contemporary world, hospitality schools in Switzerland frequently integrate the most recent industry trends and technologies into their curriculum.
  • Emphasis on Excellence: Swiss hospitality is associated with high standards and outstanding service, which can be instilled in students via rigorous training.
  • Stunning Landscape: The country’s breathtaking scenery and excellent standard of living make it an appealing place to study and live.
  • Global Setting: Switzerland’s varied and cosmopolitan environment offers students a worldwide viewpoint as well as the chance to network with peers from across the globe.
  • Benefit of Multiple Languages:Although a number of programs are offered in English, students also have the opportunity to study or hone their German, French, or Italian, thereby, boosting their professional and linguistic abilities.
  • Solid Linkages with Industry: Switzerland boasts a strong hospitality industry with a number of exclusive hotels and restaurants, offering students significant internship and career prospects.

What's the cost of studying Hospitality Management in Switzerland?

Based on the institution, the cost of studying Hospitality Management in Switzerland differs. Typically, overseas students pay between CHF 10,000-CHF 30,000 in tuition annually. Besides tuition fees, students should also set aside money for living expenses which can range from CHF 15,000-CHF 20,000 per year. All in all, the overall expense for studying Hospitality Management in Switzerland can range between CHF 25,000 and CHF 50,000 annually.

Besides tuition fees, students should also set aside money for living costs, which can be quite significant in Switzerland. Living expenses which include food, housing, transit, and individual expenditures, usually cost between CHF 1,500-CHF 2,500 per month. Thus, entailing both tuition and living costs, the overall expense of studying Business Management in Switzerland, can range from CHF 15,000 to more than CHF 100,000 annually, based on the university preference and the student’s way of living.

Eligibility Criteria to pursue Hospitality Management in Switzerland

The eligibility criteria to pursue Hospitality Management in Switzerland are as under:

  • For bachelor’s programs, the student needs to hold a high school diploma or comparable. Master’s degrees require students to hold a bachelor’s degree.
  • Excellent academic record, frequently concentrating on subjects associated with hospitality or business.
  • Submission of academic transcripts from all education institutions previously attended.
  • Scores of standardized tests viz., the GRE, GMAT may be required by certain institutions.
  • Based on the institution, the student needs to prove his/her fluency in English through appearing for English language proficiency tests viz., the IELTS or TOEFL, and/or proficiency in French, German, or Italian.
  • Prior hospitality experience or pertinent coursework may be demanded by certain programs.
  • Pertinent professional experience may be favored or needed.
  • Submission of a well-written motivation letter.
  • Letters of recommendation.
  • A curriculum vitae (CV) or resume.
  • As part of the admission procedure, an interview may be required by certain programs.
  • The student needs to provide proof of adequate funds to pay for tuition fees and living costs.
  • As part of the visa prerequisites, to study in Switzerland, the student requires a student visa, including an acceptance letter from an accredited university.

FAQs:

Q1. Is studying Hospitality Management in Switzerland costly?

Yes, Switzerland is well-known for its high living expenses and tuition fees. Neverthess, scholarships and funding opportunities are offered by a number of universities. Students should carefully organize their budget and investigate potential funding sources.

Q2. Which linguistic abilities are required?

The majority of courses are instructed in English, nevertheless, fluency in other languages viz., German, Italian, or French can be advantageous, particularly for internships and jobs in Switzerland.
