Enhance Your Career With a Business and Management Degree from Ireland

Renowned for its top-notch education system, lively cultural environment, and friendly environment, Ireland is a popular study abroad destination that provides students with a rich fusion of academic prowess and immersive cultural encounters.

Providing a mix of theoretical and practical education to provide students with abilities in business strategy, operations, and management, undergraduate degrees in Business and Management in Ireland generally take 3-4 years to complete. Postgraduate degrees on the contrary generally span 1-2 years. A number of programs focus on global business perspectives and entail possibilities for internships and study abroad experiences.

Why study Business and Management in Ireland?

One should consider studying Business and Management in Ireland for these reasons:

  • Superior Instruction: Home to a number of internationally acknowledged universities and business schools, Ireland is renowned for its excellent education standards.
  • Various Course Choices: An extensive array of business and management programs with specializations in fields viz., human resources, finance, entrepreneurship, and marketing are available.
  • English Language: Ireland being an English-speaking nation, offers the perfect setting for overseas students to hone or enhance their English language abilities.
  • Excellent Quality of Life: With welcoming natives, gorgeous scenery, and a secure atmosphere for students, Ireland offers an excellent quality of life.
  • Cultural Encounter: Beyond the classroom, thanks to a rich cultural legacy and lively social life, students are offered a well-rounded experience.
  • Possibilities for Research: Availability of state-of-the-art research facilities and possibilities for partnerships with academic specialists and business leaders.
  • Robust Economy: Ireland has a vibrant and expanding economy, providing a wealth of internship and job possibilities in international firms.
  • Possibilities for Networking: Via seminars, industry events, and alumni connections, students can develop a strong professional network.
  • Business Centre: Ireland is home to several multinational firms’ European headquarters, including major technology firms viz., Apple, Facebook, and Google.
  • Post-study Work Permit: Graduates can make use of advantageous post-study work visa alternatives, which can in turn, improve job opportunities.

What’s the cost of pursuing Business and Management in Ireland?

Based on the institution and degree of study, the cost of pursuing a Business and Management degree in Ireland differs. For undergraduate programs, non-EU students generally pay between €10,000-€25,000 in tuition annually. EU students are charged lower tuition fees, usually around €3,000 annually. On the contrary, for postgraduate programs, non-EU students generally pay between €12,000 and €30,000 per year, whereas EU students may pay between €4,000 and €12,000.

Besides tuition fees, students should also set aside money for living expenses, which can cost between €10,000-€15,000 annually, and include food, housing, transit, and other individual expenses. To assist in alleviating these costs, a number of universities offer scholarships and funding opportunities.

Eligibility Criteria to pursue Business and Management in Ireland

The eligibility criteria to pursue Business and Management in Ireland are:

  • The student must have completed secondary education that is comparable to Irish Leaving Certificate. For postgraduate programs, students need to hold a pertinent undergraduate degree.
  • For MBA or certain other programs, GRE or GMAT test scores may be needed.
  • Non-native English speakers need to prove their fluency in English through appearing for English language proficiency tests viz., the IELTS (typical minimum score of 6.0-6.5), TOEFL [typical minimum score of 80-90 (internet-based)], PTE, etc.
  • 2-3 years of work experience is required for MBA programs (differs depending on the institution).
  • Submission of a personal statement or statement of purpose.
  • Generally 2-3 recommendation letters.
  • Updated curriculum vitae (CV) or resume.
  • The student needs to provide evidence of health insurance coverage for the duration of their stay.
  • Submission of a filled-out application form and payment of application fee (if relevant).
  • The student needs to provide evidence of adequate funds.
  • To study in Ireland, the student needs to hold a valid passport.

FAQs:

Do overseas students have access to scholarships?

Yes, scholarships based on academic merit, financial need, or particular criteria viz., nationality are offered by a number of universities. University-specific scholarships (e.g., UCD Global Scholarships) and Government of Ireland Scholarships are examples of this.

What housing alternatives are available for students?

The various housing alternatives available for students include private rentals i.e. shared houses or apartments off-campus; university dormitories i.e. on-campus housing offered by universities; and homestays i.e. living with a family in Ireland.

What types of support services are offered to overseas students?

Numerous support services, including academic advising, mental health counseling, orientation programs, career services, and societies and clubs for social integration are offered to overseas students by universities.
