Fly High With a Masters in Engineering in Germany

Renowned for its top-notch instruction, cutting-edge research possibilities, and cheap or tuition-free education at a number of public universities, coupled with its rich cultural legacy and lively student community, Germany is an enticing study abroad destination.

Combining theoretical and practical knowledge, placing a strong focus on research and practical experience in different engineering fields, a Master’s in Engineering in Germany generally takes 2 years i.e. 4 semesters to complete. A number of programs are taught in English, accommodating overseas students and equipping graduates for global professions.

Why study a Masters in Engineering in Germany?

Students should think about studying a Masters in Engineering in Germany for the reasons listed below:

First-rate Instruction: Universities in Germany are renowned for their stringent academic standards and strong focus on research and innovation.
  • International Acclamation: Degrees earned from universities in Germany are highly valued globally, thereby, boosting employment opportunities.
  • Low-Cost or Tuition-Free Education: Overseas students are offered low or no tuition fees by a number of public universities in Germany, making it an economical alternative.
  • Vast Array of Specializations: Accommodating varied interests and professional objectives, a myriad of specialized programs in several engineering domains are offered.
  • Linguistic Possibilities: Although numerous programs are instructed in English, students can also learn German, incorporating an invaluable ability to their repertoire.
  • Excellent Standard of Living: Germany is known for its exceptional quality of life, secure environment, and outstanding public transit.
  • Cultural Exposure: Students studying in Germany gain exposure to a rich cultural legacy and a thriving community of overseas students.
  • Innovation Centre: Leading the world in engineering and technology, students who study in Germany gain exposure to the most recent developments and trends.
  • State-of-the-art Research Facilities: Access to cutting-edge laboratories and research facilities improves both, academic and hands-on experience.
  • Solid Linkages with the Industry: Abundant internships, partnerships, and career placements possibilities are offered by the thriving engineering industry in Germany.

What's the cost of pursuing a Masters in Engineering in Germany?

Based on whether it’s a public or private university, and whether the student is an EU/EEA or non-EU/EEA student, the cost of pursuing a Masters in Engineering in Germany can differ. Typically, very low tuition fees, usually about 1,500 euros annually or even less, irrespective of nationality are charged by public universities in Germany. Tuition fees for overseas students have completely been eliminated by certain federal states. On the contrary, higher tuition fees usually between 10,000-30,000 euros annually are charged by private universities in Germany. Moreover, students should also set aside money for living expenses, which, based on the city, can differ, but can typically range from 800 to 1,000 euros per month.

All in all, in comparison to many other nations, pursuing a Master’s in Engineering in Germany at a public university is comparatively cheaper, particularly for EU/EEA students. Although Non-EU/EEA students may face slightly greater costs, they can still take advantage of relatively cheaper tuition fees at public universities.

Entry Prerequisites to pursue a Masters in Engineering in Germany

Pursuing a Masters in Engineering in Germany requires students to fulfill the entry prerequisites mentioned below:

  • The student needs to hold a Bachelor’s degree in a pertinent field of engineering or an associated discipline.
  • He/she should fulfill the minimum GPA prerequisite (differs depending on the university).
  • Submission of academic transcripts and certifications.
  • Certain programs may demand GRE test scores.
  • Students may need to prove their fluency in English through appearing for tests viz., the IELTS or TOEFL or fluency in German through tests viz., the DSH or TestDaF.
  • Possessing pertinent work experience (if required by the program).
  • The student needs to submit a statement of purpose (SOP) or Motivation letter.
  • Generally 2-3 recommendation letters.
  • A curriculum vitae (CV) or Resume.
  • Passport.
  • Germany Student Visa.
  • The student needs to adhere to any other program-specific prerequisites.
  • Filled-out application form and payment of application fee.

FAQs:

Q1. Do overseas students have access to scholarships?

Yes, a number of scholarships viz., DAAD scholarships, Erasmus scholarships, as well as others offered by universities and external entities are available for overseas students to study in Germany.

Q2. Does studying a Master’s in Engineering in Germany require students to know German?

Particularly at the Master’s level, especially in technical fields, a number of programs are available in English. Nevertheless, learning German can be useful in everyday life and communication outside of the classroom.

Q3. Can students work while they study in Germany?

Yes, overseas students in Germany are permitted to work part-time for a maximum of 20 hours per week during the semester and full-time during semester breaks.
