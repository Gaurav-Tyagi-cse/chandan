Study Engineering and Environmental courses (sustainable) in Australia

Australia has a strong focus on both engineering as well as in environmental studies (sustainable). Studying in Australia goes beyond traditional classroom limit. With a focus on interdisciplinary collaboration, students could explore a connection between engineering, environmental science, and sustainability. This gives a holistic environment for studying and understanding the various aspects of the industry. Engineering and environmental studies in Australia is a unique branch of study. Focusing on subjects like biology, hydrology, microbiology, geology, mathematics and various engineering related subjects, this field has many unique opportunities.

Why study Engineering in Australia?

Engineering and technology have consistently been the most popularly pursued program in Australia due to its strong mining and technology sectors. The programs prepare students with various specialisations or branches of engineering such as civil, chemical, electrical, and mechanical engineering, along with specialisations in railway, mining, automotive, electronics, transportation, and telecommunications. An engineering degree from Australia opens various opportunities like further studies and employment. Typically, the universities focus on theoretical and practicality on the subject. The benefits of studying engineering are as follows:

  • Students can study engineering in Australia and gain industry insights through internship programmes
  • A wide range of courses in Australia like engineering courses from mechanical, agricultural, aerospace, electronics, and even chemical industries are offered by Australian universities to students worldwide
  • Students studying engineering in Australia can look forward to excellent employment prospects after completing their studies.

Why study Environmental course (sustainable) in Australia?

Australia is committed towards sustainability and environment. There are government policies, initiatives, programs that are specially aimed at addressing the various environmental issues. Studying environment in Australia allows one to learn from expert faculty and gain insights in innovative sustainability practices and solutions. Some reputed institutions include Australian National University (ANU), The University of Melbourne, The University of Queensland (UQ), The University of New South Wales (UNSW Sydney), Monash University, The University of Sydney, The University of Western Australia (UWA), Griffith University, The University of Tasmania, RMIT University

Curriculum and structure

Engineering Curriculum and Structure:

  • The technical knowledge and abilities necessary for developing, constructing, and maintaining systems, structures, and technologies are the core focus of engineering programmes.
  • Engineering programmes usually include core studies in mathematics, physics, chemistry, and engineering foundations including thermodynamics, mechanics, and materials science. Specialised engineering fields like chemical, mechanical, electrical, and civil engineering are also studied by students.
  • An organised curriculum comprising a series of courses aimed at constructing fundamental knowledge and abilities in the selected engineering field is typical of engineering programmes. Typically, the curriculum consists of a mix of lectures, lab work, and design projects. Co-ops and internships are additional options for integrating practical training into engineering programmes.

Environmental studies:

  • Programmes in environmental studies are centred on comprehending the social, economic, and ecological facets of human interactions with the environment.
  • Ecology, environmental science, environmental policy, sustainability, geography, sociology, and economics are frequently found in environmental studies curricula. Environmental justice, environmental ethics, and environmental management are among the multidisciplinary subjects that student’s study.
  • In comparison to engineering programmes, environmental studies programmes could offer a more flexible structure that lets students tailor their coursework to suit their interests and professional objectives. A combination of classroom instruction, fieldwork, research projects, and internships are frequently included in the curriculum. Programmes in environmental studies may also offer chances for hands-on learning and community involvement.
