All About Pursuing Engineering in France

Owing to its internationally acclaimed universities, rich cultural legacy, and strong focus on research and innovation, France is a popular study abroad destination that provides students with a varied and rewarding academic experience.

In France, pursuing Engineering generally entails enrolling in a "Grande École" or an engineering school, with programs normally spanning 5 years, comprising 2 years of preparatory coursework and 3 years of specialized study. These programs offer rigorous training and are well-known for their strong focus on both theoretical and hands-on abilities.

Why study Engineering in France?

One should consider studying Engineering in France for these compelling reasons:

  • First-rate Education: France is home to prestigious engineering schools and universities with stringent academic standards.
  • International Acknowledgement: Degrees earned from universities in France are internationally acknowledged and valued.
  • Vast Array of Specializations: Students can choose from among a vast array of engineering fields and specializations.
  • Economical Tuition Fees: In comparison to other Western nations, tution fees in France are lower.
  • Cultural Exposure: Students who study in France have the chance to immerse themselves in the rich French culture and language.
  • Scholarships and Financing: Students have access to numerous scholarships and monetary assistance.
  • State-of-the-art Research: Focus on innovation and research.
  • Global Setting: Varied student community with global exposure.
  • Economic Possibilities: Strong industrial base with a number of global corporations.
  • Internships and Industrial Ties: Solid connections with industries, in turn, offering a wealth of internship possibilities.

What’s the cost of pursuing Engineering in France?

Based on the type of institution, the cost of pursuing an Engineering degree in France differs. The tuition fees at public universities are generally lower, usually between €200-€500 annually for EU students. Non-EU students pay up to €3,000 per year. On the contrary, tuition fees ranging from €8,000-€15,000 may be charged by renowned engineering schools viz., the Grandes Écoles annually. Moreover, students should also set aside money for living expenses, which, based on the city and his/her way of living can cost between €600-€1,200 every month. To assist in defraying these expenses, scholarships and funding alternatives may be available.

Eligibility Criteria to pursue Engineering in France

The eligibility criteria to pursue Engineering in France are as under:

  • The student needs to complete secondary education that is comparable to the French Baccalauréat. For undergraduate degrees, the student needs to hold a high school diploma or comparable. For master’s degrees, the student needs to hold a bachelor’s degree in a pertinent field.
  • He/she should have an excellent academic standing and grades in pertinent subjects.
  • Submission of transcripts from all education institutions previously attended.
  • The student may need to appear for standardized tests viz., the SAT, ACT, or particular engineering entrance exams as demanded by certain programs.
  • For English-taught programs, students need to prove their fluency in English through appearing for tests viz., the IELTS, TOEFL, or comparable. For French-taught programs, students need to prove their fluency in French through appearing for exams viz., TCF, DELF, or DALF.
  • Submission of a statement of purpose or motivation letter.
  • Recommendation letters.
  • A curriculum vitae (CV) or resume.
  • An interview may be required by certain institutions.
  • The student needs to meet particular requirements based on the selected engineering specialization (e.g., physics, mathematics).
  • He/she needs to provide evidence of sufficient funds to pay for both tuition and living costs.
  • Submission of the filled-out application form.
  • The student needs to hold a valid passport and student visa to study in France.

FAQs:

  1. Do overseas students have access to scholarships?

    Yes, several scholarships including those offered by the government in France, particular universities, and private entities are available. Eiffel Scholarship, Campus France scholarships, as well as those provided by particular engineering schools are examples of this.

  2. How does the application procedure work?

    For undergraduate programs, applications are generally submitted via the Parcoursup platform or directly via the university’s website. For graduate programs, applications are generally sent directly to the university or via particular application platforms.
