Build Your Future By Studying Engineering in Malaysia

Owing to its top-notch educational system, economical tuition fees, and varied cultural setting, Malaysia is a popular study abroad destination. With a number of programs taught in English and a lively student life, Malaysia offers a distinctive fusion of traditional and contemporary experiences.

In Malaysia, Engineering programs generally offer a thorough education across varied disciplines like mechanical, electrical, and civil engineering. Undergraduate degrees in engineering generally take 3-4 years to complete and master’s degrees typically span 1-2 years. Intended to fulfill international standards while integrating local industry needs, the programs are often taught in English.

Why study Engineering in Malaysia?

Studying Engineering in Malaysia is a popular choice for the compelling reasons mentioned below:

  • Top-tier Instruction: Excellence in internationally recognised engineering programs.
  • English as the Medium of Instruction: Courses are frequently instructed in English.
  • Vast Array of Programs: Availability of an extensive array of engineering disciplines.
  • Language: English is commonly used as the medium of instruction.
  • Budget-friendly Tuition: In comparison to a number of Western nations, Malaysia offers budget-friendly tuition fees.
  • Cultural Exposure: Students who study in Malaysia have the chance to experience the country’s varied culture and way of living.
  • Contemporary Amenities: Well-furnished laboratories and classrooms.
  • Scholarships: Students have access to a number of scholarships and monetary assistance.
  • Linkages with Industry: Solid connections with local as well as global industries.
  • Employment Prospects: Expanding engineering industry that offers employment opportunities in the region.

What’s the cost of studying Engineering in Malaysia?

Based on the institution and program, the cost of pursuing Engineering in Malaysia differs. Typically, public universities may charge between RM 20,000-RM 40,000 in tuition annually for an Engineering degree. On the contrary, private universities or international institutions may charge higher tuition fees. Moreover, supplementary expenditures like living expenses which, based on the city and the student’s way of living, can cost between RM 1,000-RM 2,500 per month, should be taken into account. To assist in alleviating these expenses, scholarships and other forms of monetary assistance may be offered.

Eligibility Criteria to study Engineering in Malaysia

The eligibility criteria to study Engineering in Malaysia are as under:

  • For undergraduate programs, the student generally needs to hold a high school diploma with exceptional grades in mathematics and science subjects. Particular A-levels or comparable credentials may be required by certain programs. For postgraduate programs, the student needs to hold a pertinent bachelor’s degree with a strong academic record.
  • Submission of academic transcripts from all education institutions previously attended.
  • He/she may require to complete a pre-university program (e.g., A-Level, STPM, Foundation in Science) or comparable.
  • If the language of instruction is English, the student needs to prove his/her fluency in the same through appearing for English language proficiency tests viz., the IELTS or TOEFL.
  • For postgraduate programs, the student needs to possess pertinent work experience or research.
  • For master’s programs, the student needs to submit a statement of purpose (SOP), letters of recommendation, and curriculum vitae (CV) or resume.
  • Further prerequisites or entrance tests may apply to certain programs.
  • The student needs to provide evidence of sufficient funds to pay for tuition fees and living costs.
  • The student needs to hold a valid passport and student visa for Malaysia.

FAQs:

Q: Are there any scholarship opportunities available?

Yes, scholarships and monetary assistance based on merit or need are offered by a number of universities. Moreover, there are chances in the private sector and government scholarships available.

Q: Are overseas students permitted to pursue engineering in Malaysia?

Yes, Malaysia is well-known for its friendly atmosphere for overseas students. A number of universities in Malaysia provide English-language programs and have support systems in place for overseas students.
