An Overview of Studying Finance and Economics in the Netherlands

Being the birthplace of the stock market, the Netherlands is a fantastic place to dive into finance and economics. With its dynamic financial scene, historic charm, and a welcoming culture, studying here offers a unique blend of serious learning and lively experiences. Plus, you might find yourself enchanted by the picturesque canals and vibrant cities while exploring your academic passions!

During the 20th century, the Netherlands emerged as a global financial powerhouse, pioneering innovative trading practices and establishing itself as a key player in international finance. This set the stage for the dynamic and influential financial sector that it houses today which has a significant effect on the global market in the modern world.

Why Study Finance and Economics in the Netherlands?

  • As the birthplace of the stock market, the Netherlands has a long-standing tradition in finance, providing a rich historical context for your studies.
  • The Netherlands’ second largest city Rotterdam is a major trade, logistics and economic center of the world and is Europe's largest seaport.
  • The country is also a leader in fintech innovation, with numerous startups and tech-driven financial solutions. Studying here puts you at the heart of cutting-edge financial technology and digital finance.
  • Owing to its unique location, the Netherlands gives the student a platform to get a birds-eye view of the entire financial landscape in Europe.
  • The Netherlands is home to variety of people with unique cultural backgrounds which means the students receive an enriching educational experience while living in the country.
  • The Netherlands boasts exceptionally high levels of English proficiency (almost 95% of the Dutch population speak English), making it easy for international students to integrate and thrive without language barriers.
  • The Dutch economy offers insights into how small, highly developed nations navigate global challenges, from trade to innovation. So, studying in Netherlands will give the student a strong understanding of economic resilience and adaptability.

What’s the Cost of Pursuing Finance and Economics in the Netherlands?

For international students pursuing finance and economics in the Netherlands, the costs typically range from EUR 8,000 to EUR 20,000 per year for tuition fees. In addition to tuition, students should budget for living expenses, which can amount to approximately EUR 1,000 to EUR 1,500 per month, covering accommodation, food, transportation, and personal expenses. Including health insurance and other miscellaneous costs, the total expenses per year for international students can range from EUR 15,000 to EUR 30,000.

Eligibility Criteria to Pursue Finance and Economics in the Netherlands

  • Completion of 12th grade with strong performance in mathematics and related subjects for bachelor’s programs, or a relevant undergraduate degree for master’s programs.
  • Proficiency in English (proven through tests like IELTS and TOEFL).
  • Master’s programs may require GRE and GMAT.
  • Proof of sufficient funds to cover tuition and living expenses.
  • Submission of application form, academic transcripts, statement of purpose, letters of recommendation, and a valid passport in order to get a student visa (MVV).

FAQs:

  1. Are the programs taught in English?

    Yes, many finance and economics programs in the Netherlands are offered in English, especially at the postgraduate level. Plenty of undergraduate programs are also offered in English, however they are fewer in number.

  2. What is the student life like in the Netherlands?

    Netherlands is frequently ranked as the happiest country in the world for students. Student life in the Netherlands is vibrant and diverse. Students can enjoy a range of activities, including cultural events, sports, and social gatherings. The Netherlands is known for its inclusive and international student community, with many opportunities for social and professional networking.
