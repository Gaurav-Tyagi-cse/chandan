Everything You Need To Know About Pursuing Engineering in the Netherlands

Owing to its top-tier education system, vast array of programs taught in English, and thriving, multicultural setting, the Netherlands is an enticing study abroad destination. The country provides overseas students with a distinctive and gratifying experience because of its rich history and hospitable setting.

In the Netherlands, Engineering programs generally provide an exceptional education with a strong focus on innovation and real-world experience. The majority of undergraduate programs take 3 years to complete, while master’s programs typically last 1-2 years.

Why study Engineering in the Netherlands?

Studying Engineering in the Netherlands is an excellent choice for the reasons mentioned below:

  • First-rate Instruction: The Netherlands is home to prestigious universities with high global rankings.
  • Global Setting: Varied student community and programs instructed in English.
  • Cutting-edge Syllabus: Focus on state-of-the-art technology and research.
  • Innovative Facilities: State-of-the-art laboratories and technological resources.
  • Secure and Hospitable: The Netherlands boasts an excellent standard of living and secure cities.
  • Cultural Encounter: Rich cultural legacy and lively student life.
  • Research and Innovation: Concentrate on technology and sustainable solutions.
  • Solid Industry Connections: Strong linkages with top companies and research institutions.
  • Practical Experience: There are possibilities for internships and real-world projects.
  • High Employability: Excellent employment opportunities and career assistance.

What’s the cost of studying Engineering in the Netherlands?

Based on the institution and the student’s nationality, the cost of pursuing Engineering in the Netherlands differs. For a Bachelor’s degree, EU/EEA students pay between €2,314-€4,500 in tuition annually, while the tuition fees for non-EU/EEA students are higher, usually between €6,000-€20,000 per year. On the other hand, the fee structures for Master’s programs are typically comparable, with EU/EEA students paying approximately €2,314-€4,500 annually and non-EU/EEA students paying between €8,000 and €20,000 per year. Living expenses, which are estimated to cost between €800-€1,200 monthly, and cover food, accommodation, insurance, and other individual expenditures, are not entailed in these costs.

Eligibility Criteria to study Engineering in the Netherlands

Mentioned below are the eligibility criteria to study Engineering in the Netherlands:

  • For bachelor’s programs, the student needs to hold a high school diploma that is comparable to the Dutch VWO (pre-university education). For master’s programs, the student needs to hold a bachelor’s degree or comparable in engineering or an associated field.
  • Submission of academic transcripts from all education institutions previously attended.
  • A strong academic record, typically with a minimum GPA prerequisite, which differs depending on the university and program.
  • The applicant needs to possess a solid background in mathematics and science subjects, especially in physics and chemistry.
  • Non-native English speakers need to prove their fluency in English through appearing for English language proficiency tests such as the IELTS or TOEFL.
  • Additional criteria like essays, interviews, or project work may apply to certain programs.
  • Overseas students may need to clear additional entrance tests or assessments, as required by certain universities.
  • The student needs to submit a motivation letter describing his/her interest in the program.
  • Submission of 1 or more recommendation letters from instructors or academic advisors (as required by certain programs).
  • A curriculum vitae (CV) showcasing the student’s pertinent academic and extracurricular activities.
  • The student needs to submit a portfolio of prior work for particular engineering fields such as architecture or design.
  • Non-EU/EEA students must adhere to the visa and residence permit prerequisites established by the Dutch government.

FAQs:

  1. In which language are engineering programs in the Netherlands taught in?

    A number of engineering programs, particularly at the master’s level, are available in English. Bachelor’s programs in English are also available, nevertheless, they may be fewer that those taught in Dutch.

  2. To study in Netherlands, do students require a visa?

    EU/EEA students do not require a visa to study in Netherlands. On the contrary, non-EU/EEA students require a student visa (MVV) and a residence permit to study in the country. The host university usually helps with the application procedure.
