Know It All About Pursuing Hospitality and Tourism in Singapore

Owing to its top-tier education system, internationally acclaimed universities, stong focus on research and innovation, and varied cultural setting, Singapore is a popular study abroad destination among overseas students. Moreover, for students looking for international job prospects, the country’s central location in Asia, strong economy, and secure setting, make it an appealing option.

Well-known for its high standards and international acclaim, Hospitality and Tourism programs in Singapore provide a blend of theoretical and hands-on training in fields viz., event planning, tourism marketing, and hotel management. Hospitality and Tourism diploma programs generally last from 6 months to 2 years, whereas degree programs take 3-4 years to complete.

Why study Hospitality and Tourism in Singapore?

Studying Hospitality and Tourism in Singapore is a wise choice for the reasons mentioned below:

  • Renowned Universities: Singapore is home to outstanding academic institutions that offer specialized programs in hospitality and tourism.
  • State-of-the-art Infrastructure: Cutting-edge infrastructure and facilities that promote real-world learning.
  • Multicultural Setting: Students who study in Singapore gain exposure to varied cultures and worldwide viewpoints.
  • Standard of Living: Singapore boasts an excellent quality of life and safety, thereby, making it an alluring destination for students.
  • Economic Stability: Stable economy that offers a favorable atmosphere for learning and working.
  • Global Centre: With a robust tourism sector, Singapore is a key global hub for travel and business.
  • Innovation and Technology: A strong focus on incorporating technology and innovation in the hospitality industry.
  • Government Assistance: The tourism industry receives strong government assistance via investments and initiatives.
  • Linkages with Industry: Solid relationships with top international hospitality brands and companies.
  • Employment Prospects: There is an increasing demand for hospitality specialists in Singapore and the Asia-Pacific region.

What’s the cost of pursuing Hospitality and Tourism in Singapore?

Based on the institution and the particular program, the cost of pursuing a degree in Hospitality and Tourism in Singapore differs. Typically, Singaporean students pay between SGD 8,000-SGD 12,000 in tuition annually for undergraduate programs at public universities viz., Nanyang Technological University (NTU) and the National University of Singapore (NUS). On the contrary, overseas students are charged considerably higher tuition fees, usually between SGD 30,000-SGD 40,000 annually. Equivalent or higher fees may be charged by private universities, with certain programs costing anything from SGD 40,000-SGD 60,000 per year.

Besides tuition, supplementary expenses viz., food, housing, transit, and individual expenditures, should also be taken into account by students. In Singapore, living expenses can be relatively expensive, with estimated monthly expenses ranging from SGD 800-SGD 2,000 based on one’s way of living and housing preferences. All in all, pursuing a Hospitality and Tourism degree in Singapore can be a major financial commitment, nevertheless the country’s distinguished universities and robust tourism sector can provide significant gains through top-notch instruction and job prospects.

Eligibility Criteria to pursue Hospitality and Tourism in Singapore

The eligibility criteria to pursue Hospitality and Tourism in Singapore are as under:

  • The student needs to hold a high school diploma or comparable (for undergraduate programs). For postgraduate programs, the student needs to hold a Bachelor’s degree.
  • Submission of academic transcripts.
  • Non-native English speakers need to prove their fluency in English through appearing for English language proficiency tests viz., the IELTS or TOEFL and submit scores of the same.
  • Generally, undergraduate programs require candidates to be 17 years of age or older.
  • Applicants from unrelated disciplines are required to complete foundation or diploma programs as required by certain institutions.
  • For particular postgraduate programs, pertinent work experience is needed.
  • Submission of a personal statement or essay.
  • The student needs to submit recommendation letters.
  • He/she may also need to complete an interview (if needed).
  • Students need to provide evidence of adequate finances to pay for tuition and living costs.
  • To study in Singapore, overseas students require a valid student visa.

Based on the particular university and program, these requirements can differ slightly.
