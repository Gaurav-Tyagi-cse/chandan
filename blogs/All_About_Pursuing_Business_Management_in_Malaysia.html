All About Pursuing Business Management in Malaysia

Renowned for its economical education, excellent institutions, and varied culture, Malaysia is a highly sought-after study abroad destination. It provides a combination of contemporary facilities and vibrant cultural encounters, making it an enticing option for overseas students.

In Malaysia, business management programs provide thorough instruction in operations, strategy, and leadership, with undergraduate degrees generally taking 3-4 years to complete and master’s programs typically lasting 1-2 years. These courses are aimed to provide students with abilities pertinent to both the local and international business landscapes.

Why study Business Management in Malaysia?

Studying Business Management in Malaysia is a popular choice for the reasons mentioned below:

  • Top-tier Education: A number of universities in Malaysia offer programs that are globally acknowledged.
  • English as the Medium of Instruction: Courses are frequently instructed in English.
  • Budget-friendly Education: In Malaysia, tuition costs and living expenses are comparatively inexpensive.
  • Multicultural Exposure: Students who study in Malaysia have the opportunity to study in a varied, multicultural environment.
  • Central Location:Entry point to the growing markets of Southeast Asia.
  • Economic Development: The developing economy of Malaysia presents chances for business innovation.
  • Linkages with Industry: Gain access to domestic and international business networks.
  • Varied Business Setting: Exposure to a variety of local and global business strategies.

What’s the cost of studying Business Management in Malaysia?

Based on the institution and program, the cost of studying Business Management in Malaysia differs. Typically, a bachelor’s degree in Business Management costs approximately between MYR 15,000-MYR 30,000 in tuition annually. The tuition fees for postgraduate programs can be higher, usually between MYR 25,000 and MYR 50,000. Supplementary expenses, like textbooks, living costs, and other fees, should also be taken into account by students.

Eligibility Criteria to study Business Management in Malaysia

The eligibilitiy criteria to pursue Business Management in Malaysia are mentioned below:

  • For bachelor’s degrees, the student needs to hold a high school diploma or comparable with an excellent academic record. For master’s programs, the student needs to hold a pertinent undergraduate degree.
  • Submission of academic transcripts from all education institutions previously attended.
  • He/she must adhere to particular grade prerequisites in pertinent subjects (generally A-levels, STPM, or comparable).
  • Scores of standardized tests like the SAT or GMAT may be required by certain programs
  • If English is not the first language, the student may need to prove his/her fluency in English through appearing for English language proficiency tests viz., the IELTS, TOEFL, or comparable and submit scores of the same.
  • For postgraduate programs, pertinent work experience may be needed or favored.
  • The student needs to submit an essay or personal statement describing his/her objectives, experience, and rationale behind selecting the program.
  • Submission of recommendation letters (generally required for postgraduate programs).
  • Filled-out application form of the university or program.
  • Overseas students require a current passport.

FAQs:

Q: Do universities in Malaysia provide international programs or collaborate with foreign institutions?

Yes, a number of universities in Malaysia provide international programs or collaborate with foreign institutions. To give an example, the University of Nottingham Malaysia and Monash University Malaysia offer programs that correspond to their home campuses.

Q: Do overseas students have access to scholarships?

Yes, overseas students are offered bursaries, scholarships, and monetary assistance by a number of universities in Malaysia. For information on particular possibilities and eligibility criteria, students should contact specific universities.

Q: In Malaysia, are overseas students allowed to work part-time?

Yes, overseas students in Malaysia are permitted to work part-time for a maximum of 20 hours per week during the semester.
