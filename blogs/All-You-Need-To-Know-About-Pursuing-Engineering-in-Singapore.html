All You Need To Know About Pursuing Engineering in Singapore

Owing to its higher-calibre education system, lively multicultural setting, and central location in Asia, Singapore is a popular study abroad destination that provides students with outstanding academic programs and a vibrant, secure city experience.

Offered by prestigious universities viz., the National University of Singapore (NUS) and Nanyang Technological University (NTU), Engineering programs in Singapore generally take 4 years to complete for a bachelor’s degree. Master’s programs on the contrary typically span 1-2 years.

Why study Engineering in Singapore?

Studying Engineering in Singapore is a wise choice for the reasons mentioned below:

  1. Top-Tier Universities: Top-ranking universities viz., Nanyang Technological University (NTU), and the National University of Singapore (NUS), both well-known for the engineering programs they offer, are located in Singapore.
  2. Language: Overseas students find it simpler to adjust because English is commonly spoken as well as is the language of instruction in Singapore universities.
  3. Central Location: Located as a point of entry to Asia, offering a wealth of professional and networking possibilities throughout the region.
  4. Multicultural Setting: A varied and welcoming community that provides a rich cultural encounter conducive to both professional and individual development.
  5. Secure and Clean Surroundings: Singapore is renowned for its excellent standard of living, cleanliness, and safety.
  6. Cutting-edge Facilities: Universities in Singapore are outfitted with state-of-the-art technology and contemporary facilities.
  7. Global Technology Centre: A global technology and innovation centre, Singapore draws international corporations and startups.
  8. Cutting-edge Research Possibilities: A strong focus on research and development, with substantial investment from both the public and private sector.
  9. Scholarships and Monetary Assistance: Overseas students have access to a variety of scholarships and funding alternatives.
  10. High Employment Rate: Owing to the high level of instruction and industrial ties, graduates from universities in Singapore frequently have high employability rates.

What’s the cost of pursuing Engineering in Singapore?

Based on the institution and whether the student is a local or an international student, the cost of pursuing an engineering degree in Singapore differs. Local students generally pay between SGD 8,000-SGD 12,000 in tuition annually at public universities viz., Nanyang Technological University (NTU) or the National University of Singapore (NUS). On the contrary, international students can anticipate paying higher tuition fees, usually between SGD 30,000 and SGD 40,000 annually. Supplementary expenditures viz., textbooks, housing, and living expenses, which can add an additional SGD 10,000-SGD 20,000 per year are not entailed in these figures. To assist in alleviating these expenses, scholarships, government subsidies, and monetary assistance are available.

Entry Prerequisites to pursue Engineering in Singapore

The entry prerequisites to pursue Engineering in Singapore are:

  • The student needs to hold GCE 'A' Level or comparable with strong grades in Mathematics and pertinent science subjects viz., Physics. International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma or any comparable pre-university credentials. Polytechnic diploma in a pertinent discipline for advanced standing.
  • Some overseas students may need to appear for standardized tests viz., the SAT or ACT and submit scores of the same.
  • If English is not their first language, overseas students may be required to appear for English language proficiency tests viz., the IELTS or TOEFL.
  • Pertinent internships or project work may be advantageous.
  • As part of the application, a personal statement or essay may be required by certain universities.
  • Submission of recommendation letters.
  • Candidates my be required to attend interviews or selection tests.
  • Follow the particular application deadlines established by each university.
  • For comprehensive and precise prerequisites, visit the websites of individual universities.

FAQs:

  1. Are there any particular certifications or licenses needed for engineers in Singapore? Certification from professional organizations viz., the Professional Engineers Board (PEB) may be needed for some engineering positions, particularly those pertaining to public safety and structural integrity. Additional qualifications pertinent to their particular fields may also be pursued by engineers.
  2. Do engineering students in Singapore have access to internship or training possibilities? Yes, a number of universities maintain solid relationships with business partners, providing internships and co-op programs to give students real-world experience. Singapore is also home to a large number of companies that provide training and internship programs.
