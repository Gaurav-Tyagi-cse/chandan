Advance Your Career With an Architectural Degree from Singapore

Owing to its top-notch education system, thriving city life, and varied cultural setting, Singapore is an alluring study abroad destination. Students are offered distinctive possibilities for career advancement and global networking due to the country’s central location in Asia.

Amalgamating cutting-edge designs with sustainability, Architecture in Singapore is a vibrant field that is influenced by both contemporary and traditional elements. A Bachelor’s degree in Architecture in Singapore generally takes 4-5 years to complete. Master’s programs on the other hand take an extra 1-2 years if pursued after the Bachelor’s.

Why study Architecture in Singapore?

One should think about studying Architecture in Singapore for these compelling reasons:

  • Outstanding Instruction: Singapore is home to leading universities viz., National University of Singapore (NUS) and Nanyang Technological University (NTU) that offer well-known programs in architecture.
  • Global Acknowledgement: Owing to Singapore’s international prominence, degrees and experience are highly appreciated globally.
  • Multiculturalism: Students studying Architecture in Singapore gain exposure to a variety of architectural styles and
  • Urban Development: Students gain familiarity with high-density urban planning and design.
  • Contemporary Infrastructure: Learn in a city that is renowned for its stunning skyline and contemporary architecture.
  • Creative Design Centre: Singapore is a world leader in state-of-the-art and sustainable architecture.
  • Possibilities for Networking: Access to a robust professional network and industry events.

Leading universities in Singapore to pursue Architecture

The prominent universities in Singapore to pursue Architecture are:

  • Nanyang Technological University (NTU)
  • National University of Singapore (NUS)
  • Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD)

What’s the cost of pursuing Architecture in Singapore?

In Singapore, pursuing architecture requires a major financial commitment. Local students pay between SGD 8,500-SGD 10,000 in tuition annually for a Bachelor of Arts in Architecture at the National University of Singapore (NUS), however, the Master’s program costs approximately SGD 10,000-SGD 13,000 per year. On the other hand, international students pay higher tuition fees, usually between SGD 30,000-SGD 40,000 per year. Supplementary expenditures entail studio fees, materials, and living costs, which can increase the overall cost of education for overseas students to more than SGD 50,000 per year. To assist in defraying these expenses, scholarships and other forms of monetary assistance are available.

Eligibility Criteria to pursue Architecture in Singapore

The eligibility criteria to pursue Architecture in Singapore is as under:

  • The student generally needs to hold a high school diploma with outstanding academic performance, particularly in mathematics and science subjects.
  • He/she needs to fulfill particular grade thresholds or GPA prerequisites established by the institution.
  • Overseas students need to prove their fluency in English through appearing for English language proficiency tests viz., the IELTS or TOEFL.
  • Internships or work experience in associated fields may be advantageous.
  • Candidates need to submit a portfolio highlighting prior work and creative ability.
  • Clearing an interview or an entrance exam may be required by certain institutions.
  • Overseas students require a valid student visa.

FAQs:

  1. Do architecture students have access to scholarships?

    Yes, a variety of scholarships and monetary assistance alternatives are available. Scholarships based on merit or need are frequently offered by universities, thus, students can check with individual universities. Moreover, scholarships may be offered by government entities and external organizations.

  2. Post graduating with an architecture degree, what is the common career path?

    A number of graduates begin as junior architects or in associated positions, progressively acquiring experience and responsibility. With further expertise, they can advance to senior architect jobs, project management positions, or even launch their own businesses.
