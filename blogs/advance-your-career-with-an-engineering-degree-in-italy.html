Advance Your Career With an Engineering Degree in Italy

Owing to its rich cultural legacy, internationally acclaimed cuisine, and ancient cities such as Florence, Venice, and Rome, Italy is a highly sought-after study abroad destination. Thanks to its superior universities, vast array of academic programs, and possibilities to immerse in the art, language, and architecture of Italy, a distinctive educational experience is provided.

Italy is renowned for the robust engineering education it offers, particularly in fields like civil, aerospace, and mechanical engineering, offered by prestigious universities such as Politecnico di Torino and Politecnico di Milano. In Italy, Bachelor’s degrees in Engineering generally take 3 years to complete while Master's degrees typically span 2 years. Integrated master’s programs that last 5 years are also offered by certain Italian universities.

Why study Engineering in Italy?

Studying Engineering in Italy is a wise choice for the following reasons:

  • Internationally Acknowledged Universities: Italy is home to renowned institutions such as Politecnico di Torino, and Politecnico di Milano, that are ranked worldwide for their engineering programs.
  • Top-notch Instruction: Engineering schools in Italy provide cutting-edge, innovative curriculum with a strong focus on research and development.
  • Multilingual Instruction: English-taught engineering programs are offered by a number of universities in Italy, making them available to overseas students.
  • Budget-friendly Tuition Fees: In comparison to other European nations, Italy offers economical tuition fees, with a number of scholarships accessible to overseas students.
  • Emphasis on Innovation and Sustainability: Engineering programs frequently focus on state-of-the-art technologies and sustainable practices, which are beneficial for future-focused careers.
  • Rich Cultural Encounter: Italy's historical and cultural legacy offers a distinctive and stimulating atmosphere.
  • Solid Linkages with Industry: Strong connections with worldwide industries in the aerospace, design, and automotive sectors.
  • Strategic Location: Italy’s central location in Europe offers easy access to internships and employment prospects throughout the continent.

What's the cost of studying Engineering in Italy?

Based on the university and whether it is a public or private university, the cost of pursuing Engineering in Italy differs. Public universities may charge between €500-€4,000 in tuition annually for EU students, while non-EU students may pay higher tuition fees, generally costing between €1,500 and €5,000 per year. Private universities on the contrary are generally more costly, with tuition fees costing between €6,000-€20,000 per year.

Based on the city, living expenditures, entailing food, housing, and transit, generally cost between €700-€1,500 monthly. In comparison to smaller towns, living expenses in cities like Rome and Milan are often higher. To assist in alleviating the total expense, both EU and non-EU students have access to scholarships and monetary assistance.

Eligibility Criteria to pursue Engineering in Italy

The eligibility criteria to pursue Engineering in Italy are:

  • For undergraduate programs, the student needs to hold a high school diploma or comparable (needs to be acknowledged in Italy). For Master's programs, the student needs to hold a pertinent bachelor’s degree.
  • The student needs to have a strong academic record, particularly in Mathematics and Sciences.
  • He/she needs to submit academic transcripts from all education institutions previously attended.
  • Based on the medium of instruction, the student needs to prove his/her fluency in English through appearing for English language proficiency tests (IELTS/TOEFL certification, usually B2 or higher) or fluency in Italian (typically B2 level certification).
  • Certain universities may require the student to clear an entrance exam, like the IMAT (International Medical Admissions Test) or TOLC-I.
  • The student may need to submit a portfolio.
  • He/she may also need to submit a personal statement.
  • Letters of recommendation.
  • The student needs to provide evidence of adequate funds to pay for tuition fees and living costs.
  • Submission of the filled-out application form.
  • Non-EU students require a student visa as well as a residence permit.

FAQs:

  1. Do overseas students have access to scholarships?

    Yes, scholarships for overseas students are offered by a number of organizations and universities in Italy. A few of them are as under:

    • The Italian Government Scholarships for Foreign Students
    • University-specific scholarships
    • Erasmus+ programs
  2. Are overseas students allowed to work while studying?

    Yes, overseas students are permitted to work part-time for a maximum of 20 hours per week during the academic term and full-time during breaks. Students should make sure that the work does not conflict with study commitments.
