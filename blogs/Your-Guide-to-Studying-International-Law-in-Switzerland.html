Your Guide to Studying International Law in Switzerland

Switzerland is politically a neutral country with a strong tradition of diplomacy. It is thus host to numerous international diplomatic organizations, including the United Nations and the Red Cross. Switzerland is also renowned for its breathtaking landscapes all the way from the serene lakes to majestic Alps. The country is also celebrated for its high quality of life, robust economy, and exceptional education system. Its cities, like Geneva, Zurich, and Lausanne, offer a vibrant mix of culture, innovation, and historical charm, making it an ideal destination for students seeking a world-class education.

Why Study International Law in the Switzerland?

  • Switzerland is in the heart of Europe, making it a great central location to study in with direct access to neighbouring countries like France and Germany.
  • Switzerland hosts numerous international organizations, such as the United Nations Office at Geneva, the World Health Organization, and the International Red Cross. This makes it an ideal place for a student to get internships and get practical experience.
  • Switzerland's long-standing policy of neutrality allows it to serve as a neutral ground for diplomatic negotiations and international legal discussions.
  • Switzerland is a multilingual with four national languages – German, Italian, French, and Romansh. This multicultural nature of the country enriches the educational experience. Students are exposed to diverse perspectives and legal traditions, enhancing their understanding of international law.
  • Swiss institutions often have strong connections with international legal bodies and professionals.
  • Switzerland is known for its high quality of life, safety, and picturesque landscapes. This creates a conducive environment for students to pursue and education and career.

Top Universities in Switzerland to Pursue International Law

Swiss universities are known for their high standards of education and research. Institutions like the University of Geneva, the Graduate Institute of International and Development Studies (IHEID), and others offer specialized programs in international law. Here are some of the top universities in Switzerland:

  • University of Geneva
  • Graduate Institute of International and Development Studies (IHEID)
  • University of Zurich
  • University of Lausanne
  • University of Basel
  • University of St. Gallen
  • University of Neuchâtel

What’s the cost of pursuing international law in Switzerland?

For tuition fees, public universities charge anywhere between CHF 1,000 to CHF 4,000 per year, while private universities may charge between CHF 10,000 and CHF 30,000 per year. Living expenses, including rent, food, utilities, and transportation will be approximately CHF 1600 per month. In total the living expenses can be around CHF 20,000 to CHF 40,000 per year depending on the university selected, city of residence and lifestyle.

Eligibility Criteria to Pursue International Law in Switzerland

For Admissions:

  • Educational Background: Certificates that show the completion of secondary school for undergraduate programs or an undergraduate degree in law for postgraduate programs.
  • Language Proficiency: Demonstrated proficiency in the language of instruction (German, French, Italian, or English), often through standardized tests.
  • Additional Documents: For graduate programs, typically required documents include letters of recommendation, a statement of purpose, and a CV/resume.

For Visas:

  • Proof of Admission: Certificates that show the completion of secondary school for undergraduate programs or an undergraduate degree in law for postgraduate programs.
  • Financial Proof: Demonstrated proficiency in the language of instruction (German, French, Italian, or English), often through standardized tests.

    • Job Opportunities after Studying International Law in Switzerland

    • International Organizations: Work with institutions like the United Nations, World Health Organization, or the International Red Cross, many of which have headquarters or offices in Switzerland.
