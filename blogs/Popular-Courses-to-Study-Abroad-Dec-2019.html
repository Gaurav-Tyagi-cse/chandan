Popular Courses to Study Abroad

Overseas Education is growing increasingly popular among students and each year more students are opting to study abroad rather than in India. With an increasing number of courses being introduced, the career prospects for students also have multiplied. From the wide variety of courses available, there are a few that are popular among the students.

The popular courses to study abroad are:

Engineering

Engineering being one the most challenging degrees and jobs in the world, the courses have always been popular among students due to the wide array of career prospects. Engineers are in high demand around the world and own about 20% more than average graduates. The various types of Engineering courses that are famous among students are :

  • Mechanical Engineering
  • Civil Engineering
  • Electrical Engineering
  • Biomedical Engineering
  • Aerospace Engineering
  • Environmental Engineering

Computer Science

Computer Science courses have grown in demand with the technological advancements in the world. At the Undergraduate level, a Computer Science degree helps establish a strong foundation in Information Technology. A Master's degree introduces advanced core concepts and gives an opportunity to the students to specialize in selected area of interest. The Master of Science in Computer Science is a graduate degree Computer Engineers. Computer Science course often have curricula in coding, algorithms, programming web technology etc.

Information Technology

Information Technology or IT courses, are a budding industry that is a product of development and evolving technological advancements. IT courses and jobs are in high demand. A Bachelor of Science in Information Technology or B.Sc IT, is a Bachelor's degree awarded on completion of a course in the Information technology field that spans about three to four years. The subjects that are covered in this course are mainly, software, databases and networking and focuses on mathematical and theoretical foundations of computing.

Business Administration

The Master of Business Administration (MBA) degree is one of the most famous and sought after courses in the world. The degree is offered by more than 60% of universities abroad. The courses in an MBA program highlight different areas of Business such as :

  • Accounting
  • Applied Statistics
  • Business Communication
  • Business Ethics
  • Business Law
  • Finance
  • Managerial Economics
  • Management
  • Entrepreneurship
  • Marketing
  • Supply Chain and Operations

Medicine

Medical degrees are awarded to medical students after the completion of a degree program, that usually lasts about five years, in some cases, more at an undergraduate level and about four years at the graduate level. Medical degrees include Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS, MBChB, BMBS), Doctor of Medicine (MD), or Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine (DO) as well as additional degrees, such as a Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D), Master's degree (M.Sc).

Law

A law degree is an academic degree conferred on students that have completed their studies in law. Law degrees are generally opted for by individuals aiming at legal careers. A legal license is granted after a written examination and exercised locally while some may be, national and international as well. A LL.M. degree program includes various subjects such as :

  • Bankruptcy Law
  • Banking Law
  • Commercial Law
  • Criminal Law
  • Entertainment and Media Law
  • Environmental Law
  • Financial Services Law
  • Human Rights Law
  • Information Technology Law
  • Insurance Law
  • Intellectual Property Law
  • International Law
  • Law and Economics
  • Litigation
  • Maritime Law
  • Military Law
  • Patent Law
  • Real Estate Law
  • Social Care Law
  • Telecommunications Law
  • Trade Law

Social Sciences

Social science is a science that focuses on society and relationships among individuals within a society. This science includes subjects like :

  • Anthropology
  • Archaeology
  • Economics
  • History
  • Musicology
  • Human Geography
  • Jurisprudence
  • Linguistics
  • Political Science
  • Psychology
  • Public Health
  • Sociology

Hospitality and Tourism

A degree in Hospitality Management focuses on the study of the hospitality industry. A degree in this field is awarded either by a University or College dedicated to the studies of hospitality management, in some cases even business schools. Degrees in hospitality management are also known as Hotel Management, Hotel and Tourism Management or Hotel Administration.

International Tourism Management is a degree course with a main focus on the basics of tourism covering, cross cultural and social competence as well as leadership and professional competence.

Accounting

A Bachelor's degree in Accounting is a requirement for accountant and auditor job positions. A degree in accounting may also be required to fulfill the requirements for a membership to professional accounting bodies. Professional accounting qualifications include the Chartered Accountancy and various certificates and diplomas.

Fashion Design

A Bachelor of Design (B.Des.) is an undergraduate degree in the field of design awarded at the completion of a course that usually lasts three or four years. Bachelor of Design degrees are popular in Canada and Australia and are becoming popular in the United States as well. There are several variants of the Bachelor of Design degree, including the Bachelor of Design Arts and the Bachelor of Design Studies. Choosing from this amazing assortment of courses is not a simple task but most of these degrees have a wide range of prospects which is definitely a positive aspect of opting for these courses.

FAQs:

How do I pick the best courses to study abroad?

There are a few crucial variables to take into account while choosing the best courses to study abroad. Consider your educational and professional objectives first. Choose the courses or industries that fit your interests and goals for the future. Research universities or organisations renowned for their subject-matter knowledge. The curriculum and course options should then be taken into account next. Make sure the prerequisites, course descriptions, and syllabi correspond to your academic standing and the learning objectives you have in mind. A good mix between mandatory classes and electives that let you explore new topics or deepen your existing knowledge is important. Also take into account the surroundings and cultural opportunities. Pick a location that fits your interests and offers chances for personal development and exploration because studying abroad offers a rare opportunity to immerse yourself in a different culture. Finally, consult academic counselors, experts in your subject of interest, or former international students. Based on their experiences, they can offer insightful advice.

Are there any study abroad courses that are more suited to international students?

Yes, there are study abroad courses intended exclusively for overseas students. In these classes, the emphasis is usually on creating a welcoming environment for students from various ethnic backgrounds. They might provide specialised language assistance, cultural acculturation programs, and opportunities for intercultural communication. Furthermore, this study abroad courses frequently cover topics of interest to international students, such as intercultural studies, global business, and international relations. The objective is to develop a rich educational environment that fosters cross-cultural sensitivity and an international outlook. Among the best courses to study abroad for Indian students are Accounting, Computer Science, Design, Business Administration, Law, and others.

What are the advantages of taking particular courses abroad?

Pursuing abroad courses has a number of benefits. First off, it offers a chance for cultural immersion, giving students the chance to get to know various cultures, engage with varied groups, and develop a global perspective. Second, by pursuing foreign studies courses, students are frequently exposed to elite academic institutions and instructors, providing them with a top-notch education that can advance their knowledge and abilities. Furthermore, studying abroad encourages independence, self-reliance, and adaptability as students navigate unfamiliar situations and conquer problems, developing vital life skills. Studying abroad broadens professional chances by providing global experience, cultural competence, and teamwork abilities, enhancing job market advantage.

Are there any language prerequisites for courses to study abroad?

Based on the program and university, there may be different language requirements for courses to study abroad. While other institutions may offer language assistance or offer courses taught in English, some programs or universities may have language proficiency requirements. Before submitting an application, it's crucial to check the language prerequisites for the study abroad program you want to enrol in.

Are there any scholarships available for particular courses to study abroad?

Yes, a variety of scholarships are offered for certain study abroad courses. Scholarships for students pursuing particular fields of study are offered by numerous universities, governments, organisations, and foundations. These awards are intended to help students with remarkable academic ability, leadership potential, or particular skills in fields like STEM, the arts, humanities, social sciences, or business. Scholarships are also provided for underrepresented groups, students from poor nations, and those who demonstrate financial necessity. Investigate and explore scholarship opportunities through numerous sources viz. university websites, scholarship search engines, and relevant organisations so as to identify the best suited options for certain courses and study abroad places.

What are the prerequisites for studying abroad courses?

Based on the particular program and do you want to study in, the requirements for studying abroad courses can change. But generally speaking, there are a few basic requirements. You must first have finished secondary school or the equivalent. Your ability to communicate in the language of instruction may be tested by the TOEFL or IELTS. Additionally, you might need to fulfil certain academic requirements, like a minimum GPA or a set of preparatory classes. Lastly, you might require a student visa for the nation where you wish to study in addition to a current passport. To make sure you fulfil all requirements for your selected study abroad program, it is crucial to explore and comprehend the unique demands.

What are the popular abroad courses for students who have completed their 12th grade?

There are a number of well-liked study abroad courses after 12th that students can enrol in. Bachelor's degrees in engineering, hospitality management, business administration, computer science, healthcare, and economics are some of the popular options. Also in high demand are courses in the creative arts, including fashion, design, and film. When making a decision, students should examine their interests, professional ambitions, and the reputation of the colleges providing these courses.
