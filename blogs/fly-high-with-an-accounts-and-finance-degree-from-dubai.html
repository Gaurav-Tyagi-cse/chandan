Fly High With an Accounts and Finance Degree from Dubai

Owing to its first-rate educational institutions, central location that links East and West, and varied cultural encounters, Dubai is a popular study abroad destination. For overseas students looking for academic and career development, the city’s thriving economy, contemporary infrastructure, and worldwide viewpoint make it an alluring choice.

Accounts and Finance programs in Dubai generally include accounting principles, financial management, and investment strategies. Bachelor’s degrees in Accounts and Finance generally take 3-4 years to complete and master’s degrees typically last 1-2 years, based on the institution and the framework of the course.

Why study Accounts and Finance in Dubai?

Studying Accounts and Finance in Dubai is a popular choice for the reasons mentioned below:

  • Institutions of Higher Learning: Access to first-class universities and programs.
  • Cultural Exposure: Students who study in Dubai gain exposure to a varied, cosmopolitan setting that promotes global business skills.
  • Central Location: Entry point to markets in the Middle East and Asia.
  • Expanding Financial Centre: Dubai is a sugnificant hub for global finance.
  • Income Exempt from Taxation: Gain from income that is tax-free.
  • Increasing Demand for Experts: There is a high demand for accounting and finance specialists.
  • Possibilities for Networking: Access to a vast network of professionals and business executives.
  • Global Exposure: Acquire opportunities to partner with international firms.

What’s the cost of studying Accounts and Finance in Dubai?

Based on the institution and level of degree, the cost of pursuing Accounts and Finance in Dubai can differ considerably. Typically, undergraduate programs cost between AED 30,000-AED 60,000 annually. Postgraduate programs on the other hand may range from AED 40,000-AED 80,000 per year. While tuition is typically covered in these fees, supplementary expenditures viz., materials, textbooks, and living expenses may not be entailed. In order to acquire the most precise and recent information, students should visit the websites of individual universities or get in touch with the admissions departments.

Eligibility Criteria to pursue Accounts and Finance in Dubai

The eligibility criteria to pursue Accounts and Finance in Dubai are mentioned below:

  • For undergraduate programs, the student needs to hold a high school diploma or comparable. Particular subjects or a minimum grade may be required by certain programs.
  • For postgraduate programs, the student needs to hold a bachelor’s degree in finance, accounting, or an associated field. Pertinent work experience may also be taken into account by certain programs.
  • Based on the institution, the student may need to appear for standardized tests or entrance exams.
  • Non-native English speakers need to prove their fluency in English through appearing for English language proficiency tests viz., the IELTS or TOEFL.
  • Certain certifications viz., CPA, ACCA may be required for certain programs.
  • Submission of a statement of purpose.
  • Letters of recommendation.
  • A curriculum vitae (CV) or resume.
  • The student needs to provide evidence of his/her ability to pay for tuition fees and living costs.
  • He/she needs to hold a current student or residency visa for Dubai.
  • Submission of the filled-out application form and payment of application fees.
