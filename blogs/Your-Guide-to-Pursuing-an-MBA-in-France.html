Your Guide to Pursuing an MBA in France

By studying in France, students get access to some of the highest ranked European universities while paying some of the lowest tuition fees worldwide. France, especially Paris, is a multicultural hub, allowing students to meet and become friends with people from all over the globe. There’s also more than 1500 courses that are taught entirely in English. However learning French which will allow the student to immerse themselves in the local culture and will also make you a stand-out applicant in the job market.

The French economy is one of largest and most stable economies in the world. The economy is driven by key industries such as manufacturing, services, and technology which are bolstered by robust consumer spending. It is no wonder then that France is also home to some of the top business schools in the world.

Why Study MBA in France?

  • Quality Education: France is known for its prestigious business schools and universities offering high-quality MBA programs accredited by international bodies like AACSB and EQUIS.
  • Diverse Specializations: France offers a wide range of MBA specializations catering to various industries such as luxury, technology, hospitality, and more.
  • Cultural Benefits: Studying in France exposes you to a rich cultural experience and some of the most delicious cuisine in the world.
  • Networking Opportunities: French business schools often have strong connections with industries and offer extensive networking opportunities through alumni networks and industry partnerships.
  • Career Opportunities: France is home to multiple billion-dollar multi national companies, offering ample opportunities for internships and employment.
  • Affordable: Compared to MBA programs in some other countries, France offers relatively affordable tuition fees and living costs for international students.
  • EU Residence and Work Permit: Completing an MBA in France can help you get a residence permit in the EU which will allow you live and work anywhere in Europe.

What’s the Cost of Pursuing an MBA in France?

Studying an MBA in France is relatively cheaper compared to other countries. MBA tuition fees in France typically range from €20,000 to €45,000 for the entire program. The living expenses will depend on the city and your lifestyle. Paris, for example, is more expensive compared to other smaller cities. A rough estimate for living expenses including accommodation, food, transportation, and other necessities can range from €800 to €1,500 per month.

Eligibility Criteria to Pursue an MBA in France

  • Educational Qualification: Bachelor's degree or equivalent from a recognized university. The degree should ideally be in a relevant field such as business administration, economics, management, or a related discipline. Also many MBA programs in France prefer candidates with prior work experience.
  • Entrance Exams: Some business schools may require applicants to submit scores from standardized entrance exams such as the GMAT or GRE.
  • Language Proficiency: Most MBA programs in France are taught in English so proficiency in English is essential which can be done by providing the certificate for tests like IELTS or TOEFL.
  • Additional Requirements: Each university may also have specific requirements or preferences, such as letters of recommendation and a statement of purpose.
  • Visa Requirements: International students will need to ensure they meet visa requirements to study in France, which typically include proof of admission to an accredited program, sufficient funds to support themselves during their studies, and health insurance.

FAQs:

  1. How expensive is it to study an MBA in France?

    It is not particularly expensive to study an MBA in France. Tuition fees can go as low as €5,000 per year which is really low especially when compared to other European countries.

  2. How long do MBA programs typically last in France?

    MBA programs is France are around 12 to 24 months. The duration can vary depending on if the program is full-time or part-time.
