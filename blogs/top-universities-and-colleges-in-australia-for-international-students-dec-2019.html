Top Universities and Colleges in Australia for international students

Australia provides quality education in an environment of tolerance and eminent living conditions. The country plays host to top-notch higher education institutes that rank amongst the worlds best. Courses in Australia are compact and intensive, incorporating in themselves developments occurring in science and technology. This is a paramount consideration students take when deciding to study in Australia. Currently over 60000 Indian students study in Australia. Australia is home to over 41 universities of which, many are run by the government. These universities cater to over 3000-5000 students.

Universities and colleges in Australia offer an array of undergraduate and postgraduate programs in courses such as Information Technology, Business, Science, Humanities, among others. Students from Australian universities have undertaken path-breaking research for which they have won several prestigious awards such the Nobel Prize.

If you have chosen Australia as your study destination and are overwhelmed with the universities and colleges present, here is a list that will help you through the process:

Top Universities in Australia for International Students

University of Melbourne

It ranks #1 in Australia, and 32 globally. Furthermore, it also ranks sixth in graduate employability worldwide. The University has a distinctive curriculum that enables students to learn beyond the classroom. One of the oldest universities in Australia it continues to make an impact on various fields viz., law, humanities, culture and biomedicine.

University of Sydney

QS World Ranking places the University number one in Australia and fifth in the world for graduate employability. It also ranks third in Australia and 42nd worldwide for 2020 QS World Ranking. For various courses viz., transportation science and technology, education, veterinary science, architecture and Masters of Management, the University is number one in Australia.

Australian National University

The 2019 Times Higher Education International Outlook Indicator places ANU as one of the highest in Australia for international outlook. Furthermore, QS World University Rankings 2018/19 places the university first in Australia. In 2018 ANU had over 25500 students. The Good Universities Guide 2019 mentions that the university has one of the greatest average student-teacher ratios amongst other universities in Australia.

Intakes in Australia for Indian Students

Universities and colleges in Australia have two primary intakes in February and July respectively. The applications take 4-6 weeks to process, thus you should commence making the applications 7-8 months prior to the intake.

Upon completing their course in universities and colleges in Australia, students attain Post-Study Work Right. If you have completed your Bachelors and Masters by coursework for at least 2 years you will receive 2 years of Post-Study Work Right. Similarly, Masters by research graduates receive three years of Post-Study Work Right, whilst PhD graduates receive 4 years of Post-Study Work Right.

What prerequisites must one meet in order to enrol in a college or university in Australia?

Certain conditions must be satisfied in order to enrol in an Australian institution or university. While particular requirements vary by institution and program, the following are some typical prerequisites:

  1. Academic Credentials: Admission to best colleges in Australia normally requires a high school diploma or an equivalent certification, with precise academic requirements differing according to the program and academic level.
  2. English Language Competency: Top universities in Australia frequently need IELTS, TOEFL, or PTE Academic that indicate your academic English communication skills. Institutional and program-specific requirements differ.
  3. Minimum Entry Requirements: Certain courses may demand certain entry scores or prerequisites, such as a foundation in mathematics or physics for scientific or engineering programs.
  4. Additional Prerequisites: Interviews, aptitude tests, or portfolio submissions may be required for certain programs, viz., medical, dentistry, or nursing.

Prerequisites differ between universities, therefore check the exact requirements of your selected institution and program. Best universities in Australia for international students may require them to meet additional requirements viz., Visas, health insurance, and financial ability.

How much does attending a university or college in Australia cost?

Tuition fees at best universities in Australia vary depending on the institution, program, and your student status, whether international or domestic. In general, international students pay more than native students.

For overseas students, undergraduate programs can cost between 20,000 and 45,000 AUD annually, and postgraduate programs can cost between 22,000 and 50,000 AUD annually. Medicine and veterinary science programs typically entail higher tuition. Living expenses viz., lodging, food, transport, and health insurance can add up to roughly AUD 20,000 per year.

The values offered are estimations and may vary depending on the institution and individual circumstances. Consult the specific university or college directly for accurate and up-to-date information on tuition and other costs.
