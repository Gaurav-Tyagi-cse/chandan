Know It All About Pursuing Hospitality Management Courses In Australia

In today’s world, an increasing number of people seek unique employment prospects. Teenagers are now more open towards choosing new job opportunities. Owing to this, numerous employment opportunities have opened up. Among these, Hospitality Management is one such course. Hospitality and Tourism Management is a thriving field that presents graduates with enticing jobs all across the globe. This program emphasizes on teaching the nuances and unique aspects of running a successful hotel business. Those wishing to work in this exciting and flourishing industry look for top-notch training. This is precisely the type of training that universities in Australia provide. All the highly sought-after study abroad destinations are developing new specializations and associated courses in the field of Hospitality Management.

A number of international students are drawn to Australia, one of the popular study abroad destinations. The ‘Land Down Under’ boasts of a rich legacy and culture. There are a number of colleges and universities in Australia that offer a vast array of courses. Courses in Australia are offered across various fields of study such as Accountancy, Dentistry, Psychology, Law, Architecture, Business and Management, etc. The hospitality sector in Australia provides students with the chance to learn under subject-matter specialists in their respective fields of study and gain insights into what distinguishes the best hospitality brands.

Why pursue a Hospitality Management course in Australia?

One should think about pursuing a Hospitality Management course in Australia owing to these benefits:

  • Internationally Acknowledged Degrees: Courses and degrees in Hospitality Management in Australia are available at both the undergraduate and postgraduate levels as well. Moreover, these credentials have received the highest level of endorsement from accrediting agencies. Thus, students can be certain of the quality of their degrees.
  • Comprehensive Instruction: In addition to instilling a solid theoretical foundation, providers of Hotel Management degrees in Australia also pay close attention to key units such as Front Office Management, Asset and Property Management, Food and Beverage Management, Service Management, etc., in turn, providing a comprehensive overview of the Hospitality industry.
  • On-the-job Training: Hotel Management courses in Australia incorporate a practical training component in the form of an internship that spans six months or one semester. This internship has been designed to provide students a professional advantage in their training. It is a mandatory component of the curriculum, and the credits gained by pupils are applied to overall performance.
  • Internationally Customised Courses: Hospitality Management courses in Australia are tailored to meet the needs of professionals and students alike. Taking into account the needs of overseas students, courses have a worldwide appeal.
  • Employment Opportunities: The hotel industry around the world lacks experienced, qualified and well trained professionals. This makes Hotel Management degree holders highly sought-after in Australia and around the world.

Given the advantages listed above, studying Hotel/Hospitality Management in Australia might be a wise investment.

Prominent Universities in Australia to pursue Hospitality Management:

  • Griffith University: Griffith University has been awarded ‘Best Tourism Educator’ by National Tourism Awards in 2010. A large number of hospitality students are drawn to the Hospitality department at Griffith University to pursue its many undergraduate programs, for example, the Bachelor of International Tourism and Hotel Management, as well as postgraduate certification and diplomas in Tourism.
  • University of Queensland: The University of Queensland provides a Bachelor of International Hotel and Tourism Management degree to help students acquire successful leadership in the hotel management industry. The course integrates both, theoretical and practical experience, including initiatives such as Executive Shadowing Program, and a Tourism Regional Internship Project, aimed at helping students develop managerial skills.
  • Bond University: Bond University offers the Bachelor of International Hotel and Tourist Management program that provides vocational training to help students enter the international hospitality sector. In order to ensure that students gain valuable work experience, Bond University partners with international hotels like Intercontinental, Hilton, and Marriott.

Other Australian universities include Victoria University and La Trobe University.

What's the cost of studying Hotel Management and Hospitality in Australia?

Based on the institution and the particular program, the cost of studying Hotel Management and Hospitality in Australia differs. Typically, overseas students can anticipate paying between AUD 20,000 to AUD 45,000 in tuition annually. Moreover, living expenses, entailing food, housing, and transit, might cost an additional AUD 15,000 to AUD 25,000 per year. To assist in alleviating these expenses, scholarships or funding alternatives may be offered by certain universities. Students should also bear in mind that fees can differ between undergraduate and postgraduate programs, as well as between public and private universities.

Scholarships in Australia for Hospitality Management:

Both the Australian government and a number of universities in Australia offer scholarships to international students who study in the country. Mentioned below are some of the scholarships for Hospitality Management courses in Australia:

  • Destination Australia Program
