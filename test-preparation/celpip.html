CELPIP Overview

CELPIP, Canadian English Language Proficiency Index Program, is a general English language proficiency test. The CELPIP exam allows test takers to demonstrate their ability to function in English.

Types Of CELPIP Exam

There are two types of CELPIP test.

CELPIP (General LS)

  • Only available for people residing in Canada and USA.

CELPIP (General)

  • For people residing in other parts of the world. The CELPIP (General) test measures all English Language skills , including Listening, Reading, Writing and Speaking. It is fully computer delivered, providing test takers the opportunity to complete all test components in one sitting with no additional appointments, interviews, or test sittings required.

CELPIP Syllabus

Listening :

  • It has 1 unscored task and 6 scored parts (38 questions). There are 4 conversations and 2 monologues. All audios clips are played once only. All questions are multiple choices, with 4 options and 1 correct answer.

Reading :

  • The Reading section consists of 4 scored parts (38 questions). For each Reading part, you need to read the texts and answer the questions. All questions are multiple choices, with 4 options and 1 correct answer.

Writing :

  • It comprises of 2 tasks. Task 1 presents email prompt. In Task 2, students have to respond to a survey questions.

Speaking :

  • The Speaking section consists of 1 unscored task and 8 scored parts. Each parts has 1 task. The tasks are based on giving advice, talking about a personal experience, describing a scene, making a prediction, comparing and persuading, dealing with a difficult situation, expressing opinion, and descrining an unusual situation.
CELPIP Scoring

CELPIP is graded on a scale of 1 - 12, known as Band Scale.7+ is a good score.

CELPIP Level Descriptor

9 Effective proficiency in workplace and community contexts
8 Good proficiency in workplace and community contexts
7 Adequate proficiency in workplace and community contexts
6 Developing effective proficiency in workplace and community contexts
5 Aquiring effective proficiency in workplace and community contexts
4 Adequate proficiency for daily life activities
3 Some proficiency in limited contexts
2 Limited ability in contexts related to immediate needs
1 Insufficient information to assess
